AP Calculus Derivatives: Complete Study Guide

All differentiation rules with worked examples and practice problems

March 2026 · MathHub
← AP Calculus Hub

Derivatives are the foundation of AP Calculus — they appear in every section of the exam, from free-response questions to multiple-choice problems requiring rapid computation. This guide covers every differentiation rule you need for the AP exam, with worked examples at increasing difficulty levels.

What Is a Derivative?

The derivative of a function \(f(x)\) at a point \(x = a\) is defined as the limit of the difference quotient:

$$f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}$$

Geometrically, \(f'(a)\) gives the slope of the tangent line to the graph of \(f\) at the point \((a, f(a))\). On the AP exam, you need to be able to compute derivatives quickly using the standard rules — not from the limit definition.

The Basic Differentiation Rules

Power Rule

Rule: If \(f(x) = x^n\), then \(f'(x) = nx^{n-1}\)
Example 1

Find the derivative of \(f(x) = 3x^5 - 2x^3 + 7x - 4\).

Solution:
Apply the power rule term by term:
\(f'(x) = 3(5x^4) - 2(3x^2) + 7(1) - 0 = 15x^4 - 6x^2 + 7\)

Product Rule

Rule: If \(h(x) = f(x) \cdot g(x)\), then \(h'(x) = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)\)
Example 2

Find \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^2 \sin x\right]\).

Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^2\) and \(g(x) = \sin x\).
Then \(f'(x) = 2x\) and \(g'(x) = \cos x\).
\(\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^2 \sin x\right] = 2x \sin x + x^2 \cos x\)

Quotient Rule

Rule: If \(h(x) = \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\), then \(h'(x) = \dfrac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}\)
Memory trick: "Low D-High minus High D-Low, all over Low squared." (Low = denominator, High = numerator, D = derivative)
Example 3

Find \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\dfrac{e^x}{x^2 + 1}\right]\).

Solution:
\(f(x) = e^x\), \(g(x) = x^2 + 1\), so \(f'(x) = e^x\), \(g'(x) = 2x\).
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{e^x}{x^2+1}\right] = \frac{e^x(x^2+1) - e^x(2x)}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{e^x(x^2 - 2x + 1)}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{e^x(x-1)^2}{(x^2+1)^2}$$

The Chain Rule

The chain rule is used when differentiating a composite function — a function of a function. It is the most commonly tested rule on the AP exam.

Rule: If \(h(x) = f(g(x))\), then \(h'(x) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)\)
Or in Leibniz notation: \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{dy}{du} \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}\)
Example 4 — Single chain

Find \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\sin(3x^2)\right]\).

Solution:
Outer function: \(\sin(u)\), Inner function: \(u = 3x^2\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}[\sin(3x^2)] = \cos(3x^2) \cdot 6x = 6x\cos(3x^2)\)
Example 5 — Double chain (AP BC level)

Find \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[e^{\sin(x^2)}\right]\).

Solution:
Work from outside in: Outer: \(e^u\), Middle: \(\sin(v)\), Inner: \(v = x^2\)
\(= e^{\sin(x^2)} \cdot \cos(x^2) \cdot 2x = 2x\cos(x^2)\,e^{\sin(x^2)}\)

Implicit Differentiation

When a curve is defined by an equation involving both \(x\) and \(y\) (not solved for \(y\)), use implicit differentiation. Treat \(y\) as a function of \(x\) and apply the chain rule whenever you differentiate a \(y\) term.

Example 6

Find \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\) for the curve \(x^2 + y^3 = 5xy\).

Solution:
Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\):
\(2x + 3y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 5y + 5x\dfrac{dy}{dx}\)

Collect \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\) terms:
\(3y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx} - 5x\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 5y - 2x\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx}(3y^2 - 5x) = 5y - 2x\)
$$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \frac{5y - 2x}{3y^2 - 5x}$$

Related Rates

Related rates problems ask how fast one quantity is changing given the rate of change of another related quantity. These appear regularly in AP free-response questions.

Strategy:
  1. Draw a diagram and label variables
  2. Write an equation relating the variables
  3. Differentiate both sides with respect to time \(t\)
  4. Substitute the given values and solve
Example 7 — AP Exam style

A spherical balloon is being inflated so its volume increases at \(100 \text{ cm}^3/\text{s}\). How fast is the radius increasing when the radius is \(5\) cm?

Solution:
Volume of sphere: \(V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\)

Differentiate with respect to \(t\):
\(\dfrac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \dfrac{dr}{dt}\)

Substitute \(\dfrac{dV}{dt} = 100\) and \(r = 5\):
\(100 = 4\pi(25)\dfrac{dr}{dt}\)
\(\dfrac{dr}{dt} = \dfrac{100}{100\pi} = \dfrac{1}{\pi} \approx 0.318 \text{ cm/s}\)

AP Exam Tips

  • Chain rule first: Whenever you see a composed function, identify inner/outer before differentiating.
  • Simplify before differentiating: \(\dfrac{x^4 - x}{x} = x^3 - 1\) is much easier to differentiate directly.
  • Implicit differentiation units: Always solve for \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\) explicitly — don't leave it buried in the equation.
  • Related rates units: State the units of your answer. AP graders will penalize missing units on free-response.
  • Know the trig derivatives cold: They appear in chain rule problems, related rates, and curve sketching.
