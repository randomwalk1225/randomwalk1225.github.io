What is Paper 3?

Paper 3 is the third external examination for IB Math AA HL and AI HL students. It is unique in the IB because it presents students with an extended, multi-part mathematical problem that requires investigation and reasoning — not just recall and procedure.

FeatureDetails
Duration1 hour
Questions2 extended response questions (~28 marks each)
Total marks55 marks
Weight in final grade20%
CalculatorYes (GDC required)
Syllabus testedCan draw from any part of the syllabus, often focuses on HL-only content
The key fact: Paper 3 is designed to present problems that students have never seen before. The question itself guides you through a mathematical exploration. You are not expected to have memorised solutions — you are expected to reason carefully from what you are given.

How to Approach a Paper 3 Question

The Paper 3 question structure is intentionally scaffolded. Each question has 5–8 sub-parts (a, b, c...) that build progressively toward a deeper result. Here is the reliable approach:

Step 1: Read the whole question first (5 minutes)

Before answering anything, read both questions completely. Identify which question you are more likely to make progress on. Most students find that they can attempt both questions, but one will be more accessible.

Step 2: Answer parts (a) and (b) first

The early parts of Paper 3 questions are generally accessible — they introduce the mathematical context and establish the tools you will need. These parts often carry 4–6 marks each and should be answered with full working.

Step 3: Use earlier parts to solve later parts

The phrase "use your result from part (b)" appears frequently. Carry your result forward even if you are not certain it is correct. If you made an error in part (b), you can still score "follow-through" marks in part (c) if your method in (c) is correct.

Step 4: Don't stop at "stuck"

If you cannot complete a sub-part, write down what you know and what you are trying to find. State a relevant theorem or definition. Partial marks are awarded for relevant working, even without a complete solution.

Maximising Partial Credit

Paper 3 marking is generous with partial credit. The mark scheme awards marks for:

  • Correct method even if arithmetic or algebraic errors occur
  • Correct set-up of an integral or equation, even without evaluation
  • Correct identification of the technique to use
  • "Follow-through" from an incorrect earlier answer, if subsequent working is logically correct
Never leave a blank. Even a partially correct set-up earns marks. If you are stuck, write down the relevant formula, define your variables, and show the first step you would take. This regularly earns 1–2 marks per sub-part.

Time Management

One hour for ~55 marks means approximately 65 seconds per mark. The practical breakdown:

  • 0–5 min: Read both questions, decide order
  • 5–30 min: First question — attempt all parts, write full working
  • 30–55 min: Second question — same approach
  • 55–60 min: Review and check any incomplete sub-parts
Do not get trapped. If a single sub-part is taking more than 8 minutes, move on. Return to it at the end. The marks distribution in Paper 3 means that two questions with 50% completion each is better than one question fully complete and one question blank.

Preparation Tips

  • Work through IB Paper 3 past papers (2019–2024). These are the most relevant practice material. Analyse the mark scheme in detail after each practice.
  • Master HL-only topics. Complex numbers, induction, integration by parts, and differential equations appear frequently in Paper 3. Ensure these are solid before the exam.
  • Practice with unfamiliar problems. The best Paper 3 preparation is solving problems you have never seen before. Use STEP practice papers, AMC problems, or university entrance exam problems to build this skill.
  • Practise mathematical communication. The mark scheme rewards clear notation and logical presentation. Your working should tell a story — not just compute numbers.
