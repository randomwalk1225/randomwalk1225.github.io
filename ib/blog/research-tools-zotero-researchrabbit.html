Zotero & ResearchRabbit: Essential Tools for Your Extended Essay

Free tools for citation management, literature discovery, and research organization

Updated: March 2026
← Back to IB Blog

Writing an IB Extended Essay or any academic paper requires two fundamental skills: finding the right sources and managing your citations properly. Two free tools make this dramatically easier: Zotero for citation management and ResearchRabbit for literature discovery. This guide shows you how to use both, step by step.

Why these tools matter for IB: The Extended Essay assessment criteria award marks for proper citation (Criterion D: Presentation) and engagement with sources (Criterion B: Knowledge and Understanding). Using professional research tools demonstrates academic rigor and saves hours of manual work.
📚

Zotero — Your Citation Manager

Zotero is a free, open-source reference management tool used by over 10 million researchers worldwide. It collects, organizes, and formats your citations automatically — no more typing bibliographies by hand.

Zotero's main interface: collections (left), papers (center), metadata (right)

Step 1: Install Zotero

Download & Setup (2 minutes)

  1. Go to zotero.org/download
  2. Download Zotero for your OS (Mac, Windows, or Linux)
  3. Install and open Zotero
  4. Install the Zotero Connector browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
  5. Create a free Zotero account at zotero.org for cloud sync (300MB free storage)

Step 2: Collect Sources with One Click

The Zotero Connector adds a small icon to your browser toolbar. When you're on a webpage with a research paper, book, or article, click the icon and Zotero automatically saves:

  • Title, authors, publication date
  • Journal name, volume, pages
  • DOI, ISBN, or URL
  • The full PDF (when available)

One click saves all metadata and the PDF automatically

Zotero works with virtually every academic database: Google Scholar, JSTOR, PubMed, arXiv, IEEE, ScienceDirect, SpringerLink, and thousands more.

Step 3: Organize Your Library

Best practices for IB students

  • Create a collection for your Extended Essay (right-click → New Collection)
  • Use sub-collections for different aspects of your research (e.g., "Theory", "Methods", "Results")
  • Tag papers with keywords (e.g., "primary source", "statistics", "to read")
  • Add notes to papers directly in Zotero (right-click → Add Note)
  • Highlight PDFs using Zotero's built-in PDF reader

Step 4: Generate Citations & Bibliography

This is where Zotero saves you hours. It supports over 10,000 citation styles including:

Zotero integrates directly with Word, Google Docs, and LibreOffice

Insert citations in Google Docs

  1. Install the Zotero Connector for your browser
  2. Open your Google Doc
  3. Click Zotero in the menu bar (or Extensions → Zotero)
  4. Click "Add/Edit Citation" and search for your source
  5. When finished, click "Add/Edit Bibliography" to generate the full reference list
  6. Change citation style anytime via "Document Preferences"
IB Tip: The IB does not require a specific citation style, but you must be consistent throughout your essay. Choose one style (MLA or APA are most common for IB) and stick with it. Zotero makes this automatic.

ResearchRabbit — Your Literature Discovery Engine

ResearchRabbit is a free AI-powered tool that helps you discover related papers, visualize research networks, and stay updated on new publications. Think of it as "Spotify for research papers" — it recommends papers based on what you're already reading.

ResearchRabbit is trusted by researchers at Harvard, Stanford, Oxford, MIT, and more

Step 1: Sign Up & Connect to Zotero

Getting Started (3 minutes)

  1. Go to researchrabbit.ai and click "Sign Up"
  2. Create a free account with your email
  3. Once logged in, click "Sync with Zotero" in settings
  4. Authorize the connection — your Zotero collections will appear in ResearchRabbit
Why connect to Zotero? When Zotero and ResearchRabbit are synced, papers you save in ResearchRabbit automatically appear in your Zotero library, and vice versa. This creates a seamless workflow: discover in ResearchRabbit, cite in Zotero.

Step 2: Build Your Seed Collection

Start by adding 3-5 papers that are central to your research topic. These "seed papers" tell ResearchRabbit what you're interested in.

  • Search by title, author, or DOI
  • Or import directly from your Zotero library
  • Create a new Collection in ResearchRabbit for your EE

Step 3: Discover Related Papers

This is where the magic happens. For any paper in your collection, ResearchRabbit shows you:

Step 4: Visualize Your Research Network

Click the network view to see a visual map of how papers relate to each other. This helps you:

Topic clusters help you see the big picture of your research area

  • Identify research clusters — groups of papers on the same sub-topic
  • Find bridge papers — papers connecting two different research areas
  • Spot gaps — areas where fewer papers exist (potential EE research questions)
  • Track evolution — see how a topic developed over time

Step 5: Set Up Alerts

Stay Updated Automatically

ResearchRabbit can email you when new papers related to your collection are published. Enable this in your collection settings. This is especially useful if you're writing your EE over several months — you'll always have the latest research.

The Complete Research Workflow

Here's how to combine both tools for maximum efficiency:

  1. Start in Google Scholar — find 3-5 key papers on your EE topic
  2. Save to Zotero — click the browser connector to collect each paper
  3. Import to ResearchRabbit — sync your Zotero collection
  4. Explore in ResearchRabbit — use "Similar Work" and "All References" to find 20-30 more papers
  5. Save discoveries to Zotero — they sync automatically
  6. Read & annotate in Zotero — highlight PDFs, add notes
  7. Write your EE — use Zotero's Word/Google Docs plugin to insert citations
  8. Generate bibliography — one click in Zotero creates your reference list
