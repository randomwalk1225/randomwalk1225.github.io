How UK University Rankings Work

UK university rankings are published by multiple bodies, each with different methodologies:

  • QS World Rankings — Global comparison based on academic reputation, citations, and employer reputation.
  • Complete University Guide — UK-specific, using entry standards, student satisfaction, research quality, and graduate prospects.
  • Guardian University Guide — Focus on teaching quality, student satisfaction, and value-added scores.
  • REF (Research Excellence Framework) — Government assessment of university research quality, conducted every ~7 years.

Our ranking synthesises these sources, weighing research quality (40%), teaching and student satisfaction (30%), and graduate outcomes (30%).

Top 5 Detailed Profiles

1 University of Cambridge

Cambridge's Mathematical Tripos is one of the oldest and most prestigious mathematics degree programmes in the world. Part III (the fourth year) is the most famous taught masters programme in mathematics globally, attracting the world's top students.

Key facts:
  • Entry: A*A*A + STEP II/III (typically grade 1 in both)
  • Admissions test: TMUA (required), STEP (conditional offer)
  • Course structure: 3-year BA, optional 4th year Part III (MMath)
  • Teaching: Unique supervision system — weekly 1-on-2 tutorials
  • Notable alumni: Newton, Hardy, Ramanujan, Hawking, Fields Medallists

2 University of Oxford

Oxford's mathematics programme is known for its tutorial system, where students receive weekly one-to-one (or one-to-two) teaching with world-leading mathematicians. The depth of personal attention is unmatched.

Key facts:
  • Entry: A*A*A + MAT (Mathematics Admissions Test)
  • Admissions test: MAT (November), followed by interviews
  • Course structure: 3-year BA or 4-year MMath
  • Teaching: Tutorial system + lectures + classes
  • Unique feature: Joint degrees available (Maths & CS, Maths & Statistics, Maths & Philosophy)

3 Imperial College London

Imperial is a world-leading STEM university. Its mathematics department is particularly strong in applied mathematics, mathematical physics, and statistics. The London location provides unmatched access to industry.

Key facts:
  • Entry: A*A*A (Maths + Further Maths required)
  • No admissions test: TMUA considered but not required
  • Course structure: 3-year BSc or 4-year MSci
  • Unique feature: Strong industry links, Year in Industry option
  • Graduate prospects: Highest starting salaries among UK math graduates

4 University of Warwick

Warwick has risen to become a UK mathematics powerhouse in just 60 years. Its Mathematics Institute is one of the largest and most research-active in Europe, with EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training status.

Key facts:
  • Entry: A*A*A (Maths + Further Maths)
  • Course structure: 3-year BSc or 4-year MMath (integrated masters)
  • Unique feature: MORSE degree (Maths, Operational Research, Statistics & Economics)
  • Research: Warwick Mathematics Institute ranks top 5 in REF
  • Notable: Fields Medal recipient (Martin Hairer, research conducted at Warwick)

5 UCL (University College London)

UCL's mathematics department benefits from its central London location and long tradition of mathematical research. It offers a broad range of specialisations and has strong connections to UCL's world-class departments in physics, computer science, and statistics.

Key facts:
  • Entry: A*A*A (Maths essential, Further Maths recommended)
  • Course structure: 3-year BSc or 4-year MSci
  • Unique feature: Combined degrees with physics, statistics, management, or economics
  • Location: Central London — access to finance, tech, and consulting
  • Research: Strong in applied math, geometry, and mathematical physics

Oxbridge: What Makes It Different

Applying to Oxford or Cambridge for mathematics involves additional steps beyond the standard UCAS application:

RequirementCambridgeOxford
Admissions testTMUA (October) + STEP (June, conditional)MAT (October)
InterviewDecember — mathematical problems at the boardDecember — mathematical problems at the board
UCAS deadline15 October (early)15 October (early)
Further MathsRequiredRequired
College choiceChoose a college or open applicationChoose a college or open application
You can only apply to one: UCAS rules prohibit applying to both Oxford and Cambridge in the same year (with rare exceptions). Choose based on teaching style preference — Cambridge supervisions are more structured, Oxford tutorials allow more exploration.

Scottish vs English System

Edinburgh and St Andrews follow the Scottish university system, which has important differences:

FeatureEnglish SystemScottish System
Standard degree length3 years (BSc/BA)4 years (MA/BSc)
Integrated masters4 years (MMath/MSci)5 years (MMath)
Year 1 breadthSpecialised from day 1Broader first 2 years, specialise later
Tuition (Scottish students)Up to £9,250/yearFree (SAAS funded)
Tuition (English students)Up to £9,250/yearUp to £9,250/year
Advantage of the Scottish system: If you're unsure whether to specialise in pure or applied mathematics, the broader first two years at Edinburgh or St Andrews give you time to explore before committing. The 4-year degree also means you graduate with a Master's-equivalent qualification.
