Digital SAT 60 questions

Format & Structure

What is the Digital SAT?
The Digital SAT is the current version of the SAT, administered entirely on a computer via the College Board's Bluebook app. Since March 2024 in the US (and 2023 internationally), all SAT administrations are digital. The paper SAT has been discontinued.
How long is the Digital SAT?
The Digital SAT is 2 hours and 14 minutes of testing time, plus a 10-minute break between sections. Total time at the testing center is approximately 2.5 hours.
How many questions are on the Digital SAT?
The Digital SAT has 98 questions total: 54 in Reading & Writing (two modules of 27 questions) and 44 in Math (two modules of 22 questions).
What are the two sections of the Digital SAT?
Section 1: Reading and Writing (64 minutes, 54 questions) covers reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary in context. Section 2: Math (70 minutes, 44 questions) covers algebra, problem solving, geometry, trigonometry, and advanced math.
How does the adaptive testing work on the Digital SAT?
The Digital SAT uses multistage adaptive testing (MST). Each section has two modules. Module 1 has a balanced mix of difficulty levels for all students. Your performance on Module 1 determines whether Module 2 is harder or easier. A harder Module 2 allows access to higher scores.
What is the Bluebook app?
Bluebook is the College Board's official testing application used to administer the Digital SAT. Students download it on a laptop or tablet before test day. It includes a built-in Desmos graphing calculator, timer, annotation tools, and the ability to flag questions for review.
Can I use a calculator on the Digital SAT Math section?
Yes. Calculators are permitted on the entire Math section (both modules). A built-in Desmos graphing calculator is available in the Bluebook app. You may also bring your own approved physical calculator. Starting August 2025, CAS (Computer Algebra System) calculators are banned.
What is the time breakdown per module?
SectionModule 1Module 2Total
Reading & Writing32 min (27 Q)32 min (27 Q)64 min
Math35 min (22 Q)35 min (22 Q)70 min
There is a 10-minute break between the two sections.
How many minutes per question on the Digital SAT?
Reading & Writing: approximately 1.19 minutes (71 seconds) per question. Math: approximately 1.59 minutes (95 seconds) per question. This is more generous than the old paper SAT.
Is the Digital SAT easier than the old paper SAT?
The Digital SAT has shorter reading passages and more time per question compared to the old paper SAT. However, the scoring scale is the same (400-1600), and College Board states that scores are comparable. The adaptive format means stronger students face harder questions in Module 2.

Scoring

What is the SAT score range?
The SAT is scored on a scale of 400 to 1600. Each section (Reading & Writing and Math) is scored 200-800. The two section scores are added together for the total score.
Is there a penalty for guessing on the SAT?
No. There is no penalty for wrong answers on the Digital SAT. You earn 1 point for each correct answer and 0 points for wrong or blank answers. You should answer every question.
What is a good SAT score?
The national average is approximately 1050. A score of 1200+ is considered above average. A score of 1400+ is competitive for selective colleges. A score of 1500+ is competitive for the most selective universities (Ivy League, Stanford, MIT).
What is superscoring?
Superscoring means taking the highest Reading & Writing score and the highest Math score from different test dates and combining them. College Board reports all scores, but many colleges accept superscores. Check each college's admissions policy for their superscore policy.
How quickly do SAT scores come out?
SAT scores are typically released approximately 2 weeks after the test date, available at 8:00 AM Eastern Time on the score release day. You can view them on your College Board account.

How much does the SAT cost?
The SAT registration fee is $68. Fee waivers are available for students with financial need, covering 2 test dates plus unlimited score reports to colleges.
How many times can I take the SAT?
There is no limit on the number of times you can take the SAT. Most students take it 2-3 times. Since many colleges superscore, taking it more than once can be beneficial.
When should I take the SAT?
Most students take the SAT for the first time in the spring of junior year (March or May) and again in the fall of senior year (August-October) if needed. This allows time for score improvement before college application deadlines.
Can international students take the SAT?
Yes. The Digital SAT is available internationally. International test dates and centers are listed on the College Board website. International students switched to the Digital SAT in 2023, one year before US students.

SAT Math Content

What math topics are on the SAT?
The SAT Math section covers four domains: (1) Algebra — linear equations, systems, inequalities; (2) Advanced Math — quadratic and polynomial functions, exponential equations; (3) Problem-Solving and Data Analysis — ratios, percentages, probability, statistics; (4) Geometry and Trigonometry — area, volume, circles, right triangle trigonometry.
Is there calculus on the SAT?
No. The SAT does not test calculus. The most advanced math topics are precalculus-level: polynomial functions, exponential/logarithmic concepts, and basic trigonometry (sine, cosine, tangent).
Are SAT Math questions multiple choice?
Most SAT Math questions are multiple choice with 4 answer options. Approximately 25% are student-produced response (SPR) questions where you type in your answer rather than selecting from choices.
What is the hardest math on the SAT?
The hardest questions typically involve systems of equations with no solution/infinite solutions, complex word problems requiring multi-step reasoning, circle equations and theorems, and trigonometric functions. In Module 2 (harder version), questions can approach precalculus difficulty.
Is the SAT math harder than ACT math?
The SAT has fewer questions with more time per question, focusing on deeper problem solving. The ACT has more questions (60 in 60 minutes) testing speed and includes more geometry and trigonometry. Neither is universally harder; it depends on the student's strengths.

AP Calculus AB & BC 50 questions

Exam Format

What is the AP Calculus exam format?
Both AP Calculus AB and BC exams are 3 hours 15 minutes long with two sections:
SectionPartQuestionsTimeCalculator
I: MCQPart A3060 minNo
I: MCQPart B1545 minYes (graphing)
II: FRQPart A230 minYes
II: FRQPart B460 minNo
Each section is worth 50% of the total score.
What is the difference between AP Calculus AB and BC?
AP Calculus AB covers roughly the equivalent of a first-semester college calculus course (Calculus I). AP Calculus BC covers both first and second semesters (Calculus I and II). BC includes all AB content plus: parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, integration by parts, improper integrals, logistic differential equations, Euler's method, and infinite series (Taylor/Maclaurin series, convergence tests).
When is the 2026 AP Calculus exam?
AP Calculus AB: Monday, May 4, 2026 at 8:00 AM local time. AP Calculus BC: Monday, May 11, 2026 at 8:00 AM local time.
What calculator can I use on the AP Calculus exam?
A graphing calculator is required for Part B of the MCQ section and Part A of the FRQ section. Approved models include TI-84 Plus, TI-Nspire (non-CAS), and Casio fx-9750GIII. The College Board publishes a full list of approved calculators. CAS calculators are not permitted.
How are AP Calculus FRQs scored?
Each FRQ is scored on a 0 to 9 point rubric. Partial credit is given for correct work shown, even if the final answer is wrong. The 6 FRQs contribute 50% of the total exam score. FRQ scoring rubrics from previous years are published on College Board's AP Central.

Scoring & Credit

What is a good AP Calculus score?
AP exams are scored 1-5. A score of 3 is considered "qualified," 4 is "well qualified," and 5 is "extremely well qualified." Most selective colleges expect a 4 or 5 for credit. The score of 5 typically requires getting roughly 60-70% of the total points.
Do AP Calculus scores get you college credit?
It depends on the college. Many state universities grant credit for a score of 3+. Selective universities typically require a 4 or 5. Highly selective universities (Harvard, Princeton) generally use AP scores for placement only, not credit. Always check the specific college's AP credit policy.
Do I get an AB subscore on the BC exam?
Yes. Students who take AP Calculus BC receive both a BC score and an AB subscore. The AB subscore reflects performance on the AB-level content within the BC exam. This is useful if a college grants credit for AB but not BC.

Content & Preparation

What topics are on AP Calculus AB?
AP Calculus AB covers 8 units: (1) Limits and Continuity, (2) Definition of Derivatives, (3) Composite/Implicit/Inverse Differentiation, (4) Contextual Applications of Differentiation, (5) Analytical Applications of Differentiation, (6) Integration, (7) Differential Equations, (8) Applications of Integration.
What extra topics are on AP Calculus BC?
Beyond all AB content, BC adds two major units: Unit 9 — Parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, and arc length. Unit 10 — Infinite sequences and series, convergence tests, Taylor and Maclaurin series, power series, and Lagrange error bound. BC also includes integration by parts, improper integrals, logistic models, and Euler's method.
Should I take AP Calculus AB or BC?
If you are comfortable with precalculus and want a challenge, take BC. BC covers AB content plus additional topics, and you receive an AB subscore. If you prefer a slower pace or haven't completed a strong precalculus course, start with AB. Many STEM-oriented students aiming for competitive colleges take BC.
What math should I know before AP Calculus?
You need strong fluency in algebra, trigonometry, and precalculus. Specifically: polynomial and rational functions, exponential and logarithmic functions, trigonometric functions (all 6), limits concept, and function composition. Most students take Precalculus or Pre-AP Precalculus the year before.
How many hours should I study for the AP Calculus exam?
If you've completed the full AP Calculus course, 40-60 hours of focused review over 4-6 weeks is typical. Focus on: past FRQs (published on AP Central), timed practice tests, and the specific topics where you're weakest. The College Board releases FRQs with scoring rubrics from every previous year.

AP Statistics 25 questions

What is the AP Statistics exam format?
The AP Statistics exam is 3 hours long:
SectionQuestionsTimeWeight
I: MCQ40 questions90 min50%
II: FRQ6 questions90 min50%
The FRQ section includes 5 short-answer questions and 1 investigative task (a longer, multi-part question). Graphing calculators are permitted throughout the entire exam.
When is the 2026 AP Statistics exam?
Thursday, May 7, 2026 at 12:00 PM (afternoon session).
What topics are on AP Statistics?
AP Statistics covers 9 units across 4 big ideas: (1) Exploring Data — distributions, summary statistics, comparing distributions; (2) Sampling and Experimentation — sampling methods, experimental design, bias; (3) Anticipating Patterns — probability, random variables, binomial/geometric/normal distributions, sampling distributions; (4) Statistical Inference — confidence intervals and significance tests for proportions and means, chi-square tests, linear regression inference.
Is AP Statistics easier than AP Calculus?
AP Statistics involves less computational complexity than AP Calculus and doesn't require advanced math prerequisites. However, it requires strong reading comprehension, the ability to interpret data in context, and clear written explanations on FRQs. Students who are strong writers may find Statistics more approachable; students who prefer computational math may prefer Calculus.
What calculator do I need for AP Statistics?
A graphing calculator is recommended. The TI-84 Plus is the most commonly used. You need to know how to perform 1-variable stats, regression, normal distribution calculations (normalcdf, invNorm), binomial and geometric probabilities, confidence intervals, and hypothesis tests on your calculator.
What math do I need before AP Statistics?
AP Statistics requires Algebra 2 as a prerequisite. Unlike AP Calculus, it does not require precalculus or trigonometry. The math used is primarily arithmetic and basic algebra; the challenge is in interpretation and reasoning rather than computation.
Can I take AP Statistics and AP Calculus in the same year?
Yes. Many students take both in the same year, especially juniors and seniors. The exams are on different dates. The two courses cover different mathematical areas with little overlap, so studying for both is manageable.

AMC 8 / 10 / 12 40 questions

AMC 8

What is the AMC 8?
The AMC 8 is a 25-question, 40-minute multiple-choice math competition for students in grade 8 and below (age 14.5 or younger). It is organized by the Mathematical Association of America (MAA). Topics include counting, probability, estimation, spatial visualization, number theory, and algebra. Calculators are not permitted.
How is the AMC 8 scored?
The AMC 8 is scored with 1 point per correct answer and 0 for wrong or blank answers. Maximum score is 25. There is no penalty for guessing.
When is the AMC 8 held?
The AMC 8 is typically held in January. For the 2025-2026 cycle, the testing window is January 22-30, 2026.
Does the AMC 8 qualify for AIME?
No. The AMC 8 is a standalone competition. Only the AMC 10 and AMC 12 can qualify students for the AIME.

AMC 10 & AMC 12

What is the AMC 10 / AMC 12 format?
Both the AMC 10 and AMC 12 have 25 multiple-choice questions with 5 answer choices each, to be completed in 75 minutes. The AMC 10 is for students in grade 10 and below; the AMC 12 is for students in grade 12 and below. Calculators are not permitted.
How is the AMC 10/12 scored?
Scoring: 6 points for each correct answer, 1.5 points for each blank, and 0 points for each wrong answer. A student who answers nothing receives 25 × 1.5 = 37.5 points. The maximum score is 150 (25 × 6).
What are the 2025-2026 AMC 10/12 test dates?
AMC 10A / AMC 12A: November 5, 2025. AMC 10B / AMC 12B: November 13, 2025. Students may take both A and B versions but cannot take AMC 10 and AMC 12 on the same day.
What are the AIME qualification cutoffs for 2025-2026?

At minimum, the top 2.5% of AMC 10 participants and top 5% of AMC 12 participants qualify for AIME.
What is the difference between AMC 10 and AMC 12?
AMC 10 excludes topics beyond Algebra 2 (no precalculus, logarithms, or trigonometry beyond basic right triangle trig). AMC 12 includes all math through precalculus. Both have the same format (25 questions, 75 minutes) and scoring system. AMC 12 questions are generally harder, but the AIME cutoff is typically lower for AMC 12.
Can I take both AMC 10 and AMC 12?
Not on the same day. But you can take AMC 10A and AMC 12B (or vice versa) since A and B are on different dates. Your highest qualifying score from any AMC is used for AIME qualification.
What topics are on the AMC 10?
AMC 10 covers: arithmetic, algebra (through Algebra 2), counting and probability, number theory, and geometry (plane and basic coordinate geometry). It does not require knowledge of logarithms, trigonometric identities, or advanced precalculus.
How should I prepare for the AMC?
Practice with past AMC problems (available free on Art of Problem Solving wiki). Focus on: number theory, counting/probability, and geometry problem-solving techniques. Books like "Introduction to Counting and Probability" and "Introduction to Number Theory" by AoPS are popular. Most importantly, practice under timed conditions (75 minutes for 25 questions).

AIME 20 questions

What is the AIME?
The AIME (American Invitational Mathematics Examination) is the second round of the AMC competition series. It is a 15-question, 3-hour exam for students who qualify through the AMC 10 or AMC 12. Each answer is an integer from 000 to 999.
When is the 2026 AIME?
AIME I: February 5, 2026. AIME II: February 11, 2026. Students take only one version (determined by their school's registration).
How is the AIME scored?
1 point per correct answer, 0 points for wrong or blank answers. Maximum score is 15. There is no partial credit and no penalty for guessing. All answers are integers between 000 and 999.
How do you qualify for USA(J)MO from AIME?
Starting in 2025-2026, the USAMO/USAJMO Index is calculated as: (AIME score × 20) + AMC score. The MAA selects approximately the top 11% of AIME participants for USAMO or USAJMO based on these index scores. Exact cutoffs vary by year and are published after the AIME.
What is the difference between USAMO and USAJMO?
USAJMO (USA Junior Mathematical Olympiad) is for students who qualified through AMC 10. USAMO (USA Mathematical Olympiad) is for students who qualified through AMC 12. Both are 2-day, 9-hour proof-based competitions with 6 problems. Top USAMO performers are selected for the US team at the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO).
What is a good AIME score?
The median AIME score is typically around 3-5. A score of 7+ is strong. A score of 10+ is exceptional and likely qualifies for USA(J)MO depending on the year and your AMC score. The average AIME qualifier answers about 4-5 questions correctly.
Are calculators allowed on the AIME?
No. Calculators, rulers, compasses, and protractors are not permitted on the AIME. Only scratch paper, pencils, and erasers are allowed.
What math topics appear on the AIME?
The AIME tests: algebra (polynomials, complex numbers, sequences), geometry (coordinate, 3D, trigonometric), number theory (modular arithmetic, divisibility, Diophantine equations), and combinatorics (advanced counting, probability, recursion). Problems require creative problem-solving beyond standard curriculum.

PSAT / National Merit 30 questions

What is the PSAT/NMSQT?
The PSAT/NMSQT (Preliminary SAT/National Merit Scholarship Qualifying Test) serves as both practice for the SAT and the qualifying exam for the National Merit Scholarship Program. It has the same format as the Digital SAT: 2 hours 14 minutes, 98 questions, adaptive testing via Bluebook.
What is the PSAT score range?
The PSAT is scored 160 to 1520 (vs the SAT's 200 to 1600). The scores are on the same scale: a 1200 on the PSAT represents the same skill level as a 1200 on the SAT.
How is the National Merit Selection Index calculated?
The Selection Index (SI) = (2 × Reading/Writing score + Math score) ÷ 10. The SI ranges from 48 to 228. This index is used to determine Commended Students and Semifinalists.
What is the National Merit Commended Student cutoff?
For the Class of 2026, the Commended Student cutoff is a Selection Index of 210. Approximately 34,000 students nationwide receive Commended recognition.
How does National Merit Semifinalist selection work?
Approximately 16,000 students (less than 1% of high school seniors) are named Semifinalists based on state-specific cutoffs. The cutoffs vary by state because selection is proportional to each state's share of graduating seniors.
What are the highest and lowest National Merit Semifinalist cutoffs?
For the Class of 2026: Highest: Massachusetts, New Jersey, and DC at 225. Lowest: New Mexico, North Dakota, West Virginia, and Wyoming at 210 (same as Commended level). The range reflects differences in test participation and performance across states.
What is the National Merit Semifinalist cutoff for California?
For the Class of 2026, the California Semifinalist cutoff is 224.
What is the National Merit Semifinalist cutoff for Texas?
For the Class of 2026, the Texas Semifinalist cutoff is 222.
What is the National Merit Semifinalist cutoff for New York?
For the Class of 2026, the New York Semifinalist cutoff is 223.
What is the National Merit Semifinalist cutoff for Florida?
For the Class of 2026, the Florida Semifinalist cutoff is 219.
How do Semifinalists become Finalists?
Semifinalists must: (1) complete a detailed scholarship application by February of senior year, (2) maintain high academic standing, (3) take the SAT and earn a score that confirms the PSAT performance, and (4) receive a school official's endorsement. About 95% of Semifinalists advance to Finalist status (~15,000 students).
What scholarships do National Merit Finalists receive?
Finalists are eligible for three types of scholarships: (1) National Merit $2,500 Scholarships — about 2,500 one-time awards. (2) Corporate-sponsored scholarships — for children of employees of sponsoring companies. (3) College-sponsored scholarships — many universities offer full-tuition or significant scholarships to Finalists (e.g., University of Alabama, University of Oklahoma, University of Central Florida).
When should I take the PSAT?
The PSAT is typically administered in October of junior year (11th grade). This is the only administration that counts for National Merit. Students may also take it in 10th or 9th grade for practice, but only the 11th-grade score qualifies for National Merit.
How does the PSAT differ from the SAT in difficulty?
The PSAT has the same structure and timing as the SAT (98 questions, 2h14m, adaptive). However, questions are slightly easier overall, particularly in advanced math and geometry/trigonometry. The PSAT includes more problem-solving and data analysis questions relative to the SAT.
