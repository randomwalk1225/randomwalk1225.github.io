Mastering the AP Calculus Exam with Past Papers

The AP Calculus AB and BC exams are designed to test your understanding of differential and integral calculus. Success on these exams depends not only on your mathematical knowledge but also on your familiarity with the Free Response Question (FRQ) format. College Board typically releases the FRQ section of each year's exam, and these papers are the most valuable resources for your preparation.

How to Practice FRQs

  • Time yourself: You have 90 minutes for 6 questions (approx. 15 min per question).
  • Show all work: You can lose points for the correct answer if you don't show the setup!
  • Use the Rubric: Always check the 'Scoring Guidelines' to see exactly how points are awarded.

AB vs. BC Differences

While Calculus AB covers Units 1–8, Calculus BC includes Units 9 (Parametric, Polar, Vector) and Unit 10 (Infinite Series). BC students will also see more advanced integration techniques and Euler's Method in their exams.
