AP Calculus AB: Solving a Separable Differential Equation, Step by Step

The separable differential equation is one of the most predictable free-response questions on the AP Calculus AB exam — and one of the most heavily penalized when done loosely. Here is a complete worked example in the exact style of the FRQ, with every algebra step shown and every point-losing mistake flagged.

Written and solved by Daniel Park, math educator (MathHub). Last updated June 12, 2026. All work below is original and fully checkable by hand.

The College Board releases each year's free-response questions publicly, and on the AB exam a separable differential equation appears almost every year. The numbers change; the structure does not. If you can reproduce the five steps below cleanly, you can answer essentially any version of this question. We will use an original problem so that every number is verifiable, then point out exactly where the scoring guidelines award — and withhold — points.

Problem (AB-style, non-calculator)

A cup of coffee is poured at a temperature of \(90^\circ\mathrm{C}\) into a room held at a constant \(20^\circ\mathrm{C}\). Let \(T(t)\) be the temperature of the coffee, in degrees Celsius, \(t\) minutes after it is poured. The temperature changes at a rate modeled by

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = -\tfrac{1}{10}\,(T - 20), \qquad T(0) = 90. \]

(a) Use the differential equation to explain why the coffee is cooling, and identify the temperature the model approaches for large \(t\).
(b) Find an expression for \(T(t)\) by solving the differential equation with the given initial condition.
(c) Find the temperature of the coffee at \(t = 10\) minutes.
(d) Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{t\to\infty} T(t)\) and interpret it.

Part (a) — Reading the equation before solving it

At \(t = 0\), \(T = 90\), so \(T - 20 = 70 > 0\). Therefore

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = -\tfrac{1}{10}(70) = -7 < 0. \]

The rate of change is negative, so the temperature is decreasing — the coffee is cooling. The factor \((T - 20)\) shrinks as \(T\) falls toward \(20\), so the cooling slows down. When \(T = 20\), \(\frac{dT}{dt} = 0\) and the temperature stops changing: \(20^\circ\mathrm{C}\) (room temperature) is the value the model approaches.

Part (b) — Separation of variables

This is where points are won or lost. The equation is separable because the right side factors as a function of \(T\) times a constant. Move every \(T\) to the left and every \(t\) to the right before integrating.

1Separate the variables:
\[ \frac{dT}{T - 20} = -\tfrac{1}{10}\,dt. \]
2Integrate both sides:
\[ \int \frac{dT}{T - 20} = \int -\tfrac{1}{10}\,dt \;\Longrightarrow\; \ln|T - 20| = -\tfrac{1}{10}\,t + C. \]
Mistake that costs a point
Forgetting the constant of integration \(+C\) here. Without it you cannot use the initial condition, and the scoring guidelines reserve a specific point for producing a constant and solving for it. Add \(+C\) the instant you integrate.
3Solve for \(T\). Exponentiate both sides:
\[ |T - 20| = e^{-t/10 + C} = e^{C}\,e^{-t/10}. \]

Since the coffee starts above room temperature and the model never crosses \(20\), \(T - 20 > 0\), so we drop the absolute value and write the positive constant \(e^{C}\) as a single constant \(A\):

\[ T - 20 = A\,e^{-t/10} \;\Longrightarrow\; T = 20 + A\,e^{-t/10}. \]
4Apply the initial condition \(T(0) = 90\):
\[ 90 = 20 + A\,e^{0} = 20 + A \;\Longrightarrow\; A = 70. \]
5Write the particular solution:
\( T(t) = 20 + 70\,e^{-t/10} \)

Part (c) — Temperature at \(t = 10\)

Substitute \(t = 10\):

\[ T(10) = 20 + 70\,e^{-10/10} = 20 + 70\,e^{-1}. \]

Using \(e^{-1} \approx 0.3679\):

\[ T(10) \approx 20 + 70(0.3679) = 20 + 25.75 = 45.75. \]
After 10 minutes the coffee is about \(45.75^\circ\mathrm{C}\) (exact form: \(20 + 70e^{-1}\)).
Exam habit
On the AB exam, leave the exact form \(20 + 70e^{-1}\) unless a decimal is requested. A correct exact expression always earns the point; a rounded decimal can lose it if rounded too early.

Part (d) — Long-run behavior

As \(t \to \infty\), the exponential \(e^{-t/10} \to 0\), so

\[ \lim_{t\to\infty} T(t) = 20 + 70\cdot 0 = 20. \]
The coffee's temperature approaches \(20^\circ\mathrm{C}\), the room temperature — exactly the equilibrium we predicted from the equation in part (a) without solving it.

The five steps to memorize

Every separable differential equation FRQ reduces to the same skeleton:

  1. Separate — all of one variable on each side. (No separation, no points.)
  2. Integrate both sides and write \(+C\) immediately.
  3. Solve for the dependent variable, folding constants into one constant.
  4. Apply the initial condition to find that constant.
  5. State the particular solution and answer the specific question asked.
