3. Analysis of the Most Challenging Problem: FRQ #6

The Problem: Taylor Series with Lagrange Error Bound.

Research Insights: This problem was the 2019 "rank-orderer." It required students to handle symbolic manipulation of a function defined by a series and then rigorously justify an error bound.

  • Common Pitfall 1: Difficulty in deriving the Maclaurin series for \\( \\( f(x) \\) = \frac{h(x)-1}{x} \\) from the series of \\( h(x) \\).
  • Common Pitfall 2: Incorrectly identifying the "M" value for the Lagrange Error Bound or confusing it with the Alternating Series Error Bound.
  • Common Pitfall 3: Failing to integrate the series term-by-term correctly when defined within an integral function \\( h(x) \\).

4. Mark Scheme Insights

Scoring for BC-specific topics in 2019 showed a trend toward requiring explicit justification for convergence and error bounds. Key takeaways include:

  • Taylor Polynomials: Points were awarded separately for the first few terms. Even if the general term was wrong, students could earn partial credit for correctly calculating \\( f(0), f'(0) \\), etc.
  • Improper Integrals: Using "infinity" as a limit of integration in the evaluation step (\( \dots|_1^\infty \)) without a limit notation (\( \lim_{b \to \infty} \)) often led to a loss of the "communication" point.
  • Error Bound Justification: The "Justification" point for the error bound required a clear numerical comparison (e.g., \( \frac{9}{20} \leq 0.45 \)).

5. Strategic Recommendations

Preparation should focus on the "Upper Tier" of BC difficulty:

  • Master Lagrange Error Bound: This remains the most conceptually difficult part of the series unit. Practice finding the maximum value of the derivative on a given interval.
  • Polar Area Fluency: Be comfortable finding intersection points of polar curves using a calculator to set correct limits of integration.
  • Symbolic Manipulation: Practice creating new series from known ones via substitution, multiplication, and term-by-term differentiation/integration.
