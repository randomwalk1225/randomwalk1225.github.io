The Escalator Problem: 2018 AP Calculus AB FRQ Analysis

Analyzing the 2018 AB exam, featuring the infamous escalator rate problem and the heavy integration of theorems like MVT and IVT.

Quick Summary

  • Difficulty: Moderate. Q1 was straightforward, but Q5 required strong algebraic precision.
  • Focus Area: High importance on Theorem justifications (MVT, IVT, EVT).
  • Calculator Strategy: Numeric differentiation was key for particle motion analysis in Q2.

1. Conceptual Shifts: Beyond Formulas to Formal Proofs

The 2018 AB exam was a showcase for formal calculus theorems. Nearly every question required a "Yes/No" followed by a justification using a specific theorem. It's no longer enough to see that a slope exists; students must explicitly state: "Since \\( f \\) is continuous on \\( [a,b] \\) and differentiable on \\( (a,b) \\), by the Mean Value Theorem..."

We also saw a shift in Accumulation Functions. Question 3 featured a piecewise-defined derivative graph \\( f' \\), requiring students to calculate areas and handle initial conditions \\( f(a)=C \\) with high accuracy.

2. Question Breakdown: Moving Parts

  • Q1 (Escalator Rates): A classic "Rate In/Rate Out" scenario. The "total number of people who get off the escalator" was a direct integration of \\( L(t) \\).
  • Q3 (Graph of \\( f' \\)): Calculating the absolute maximum of \\( f \\) on a closed interval \\( [-5,5] \\). Students must test both the critical points (where \\( f'=0 \\)) and the endpoints.
  • Q5 (Differential Equation): Solving \\( \frac{\\( dy \\)}{\\( dx \\)} = \frac{y-1}{x^2} \\). This required separation of variables, where the \\( |y-1| \\) in the natural log led to many "forgotten constant" errors.
  • Q6 (Function Continuity): A multipart question asking for the value of \\( k \\) that makes a function continuous, followed by a limit calculation using L'Hôpital's Rule.

3. Strategic Recommendations

Practice Justification Templates. Memorize the exact wording required for the MVT, IVT, and the First/Second Derivative Tests. The 2018 exam showed that points are frequently lost for "incomplete reasoning" even if the final numerical answer is correct. Additionally, ensure you can solve separable differential equations involving logs and exponential growth effortlessly.
