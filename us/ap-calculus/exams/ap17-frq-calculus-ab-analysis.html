3. Analysis of the Most Challenging Problem: FRQ #3

The Problem: Particle Motion and Position Analysis.

Research Insights: This problem was the 2017 "rank-orderer." It required students to not only find the position of a particle by integrating velocity but also to compare the motion of two distinct particles and determine when they move in the same direction.

  • Common Pitfall 1: Forgetting the initial condition (starting position) when calculating the total displacement vs. final position.
  • Common Pitfall 2: Confusion over "speed increasing/decreasing" which requires comparing the signs of both velocity and acceleration.
  • Common Pitfall 3: Misinterpreting the graph of a derivative for particle \\( P \\) while being given an algebraic velocity for particle \\( Q \\).

4. Mark Scheme Insights

Scoring for AB topics in 2017 showed a trend toward requiring specific justifications for extrema and motion trends. Key takeaways include:

  • Accumulation Setup: In Questions 1 and 2, 1 point was typically awarded for the integral \\( \int R(t) dt \\) and 1 point for correctly including the initial amount \\( A(0) \\).
  • Separable DEQs: In Question 6, the separation of variables (\\( 1/y^2 \\( dy \\) = \dots \\)) was worth 1-2 points alone. Failing to separate variables resulted in 0 out of 5 points for the entire part.
  • Washer Method: For volume problems, points were heavily weighted toward the correct radius definitions (\\( R_{outer} \\) and \\( r_{inner} \\)) when rotating around a line other than an axis.

5. Strategic Recommendations

Preparation should focus on these high-frequency AB challenges:

  • Master the Candidates Test: Be ready to find absolute extrema on a closed interval by checking both critical points and endpoints.
  • Rate In/Rate Out Mastery: Practice interpreting \\( A'(t) = R_{in}(t) - R_{out}(t) \\) and understanding that \\( A'(t)=0 \\) is where the maximum or minimum amount occurs.
  • Particle Motion Vocabulary: Know the difference between displacement, total distance traveled, and position.
