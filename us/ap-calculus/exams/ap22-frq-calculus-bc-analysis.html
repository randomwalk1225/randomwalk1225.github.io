02. Killer Problem Analysis

FRQ #6: Sequence and Series Convergence (Non-Calculator)

This question asked for the Radius of Convergence and the Interval of Convergence for a power series. It required a flawless execution of the Ratio Test followed by endpoint testing.

Key Pitfalls:

Endpoint Analysis: Many students found the radius but failed to test endpoints using the p-Series or Alternating Series tests. Additionally, skipping absolute values in the Ratio Test setup (\( \lim_{n \to \infty} |a_{n+1}/a_n| < 1 \)) resulted in lost setup points.

03. Mark Scheme Insights

Precision in notation and justification was strictly rewarded in the 2022 mark scheme:

  • Ratio Test Rigor: Limit notation and absolute values are non-negotiable for full credit.
  • Endpoint Justification: You must explicitly state which test you are using (e.g., "By the Alternating Series Test...") to justify convergence at an endpoint.
  • Integration by Parts: In multi-step integrals, one point is often awarded solely for the correct assignment of \( u \) and \( dv \).

04. Strategic Advice for BC Success

For BC candidates, mastery of series manipulation is the key differentiator:

  • Series Mastery: Master the Taylor Series for \( e^x \), \( \sin x \), and \( \cos x \) to enable quick manipulation without re-deriving.
  • Algebraic Organization: Be extremely organized with algebraic simplification of factorials and powers of \( x \) during Ratio Test applications.
  • Reflexive Endpoint Analysis: Practice "Endpoint Analysis" until it becomes a reflex to avoid missing these critical 2-point steps.
