02. Killer Problem Analysis

FRQ #5: Slope Fields and Diff Equations (Non-Calculator)

This was the "decider" question for 2022. It required sketching a slope field and then finding a particular solution \( y = f(x) \) to a separable differential equation.

Key Pitfalls:

Separation of Variables: Students who failed to move all \( y \) terms to the \( dy \) side in the first step received 0 out of 5 points. Additionally, forgetting the constant of integration (\( +C \)) immediately after integrating prevented correct use of the initial condition.

03. Mark Scheme Insights

The 2022 mark scheme prioritized structural accuracy in calculus operations:

  • The Separation Point: The first point in a separable DE problem is for the separation itself. No separation = No points.
  • The \( +C \) Rule: Forgetting \( +C \) caps the problem at 1 or 2 points maximum.
  • Justification Rigor: Vague statements like "the graph turns" earn zero points. Students must use specific tests (First or Second Derivative Test).

04. Strategic Advice for High Scorers

To reach a 5, focus on "Calculus Grammar" and verification:

  • Grammar of Justification: Use precise phrasing: "Since \( f'(x) \) changes from positive to negative at \( x = c \)..." to justify relative extrema.
  • Double-Check Separation: Verify your separation of variables twice before proceeding with integration; it is the most costly mistake on the exam.
  • Interpretation in Context: Practice interpreting derivatives as rates of change with specific units (e.g., people per hour) to ensure interpretation points.
