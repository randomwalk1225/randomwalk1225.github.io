April 2019 SAT Math Research Analysis

A deep dive into statistical inference, data interpretation patterns, and experimental design observed in the Spring 2019 administration.

Executive Summary

The April 2019 SAT administration featured a significant emphasis on the validity of statistical claims and data literacy. Section 4 required efficient information extraction from complex tables and a deep understanding of experimental design.

2019 global data shows an average Math score of 528, with the April sitting (School Day) focusing heavily on real-world utility and inference-based problem solving.

01. Conceptual Shifts: Statistical Generalizability

The April 2019 exam featured a significant emphasis on the validity of statistical claims. Students were asked whether a study’s findings could be generalized to a larger population based on the sample selection method. This conceptual layer rewards students who understand research design over those who only know how to calculate mean and median.

This "inference-based" math is a hallmark of School Day SATs (like the April administration), focusing on real-world utility and data literacy.

02. Topic Distribution: Data Sets and Scatterplots

Problem Solving and Data Analysis was the largest domain by weight in this sitting. Scatterplots and "Line of Best Fit" were tested through multiple lenses, including interpretation of the slope as a "unit increase."

  • Probability: Two-way tables were used to test conditional probability, where the "denominator" was restricted by a specific condition.
  • Data Displays: Box-plots and Dot-plots required comparative analysis of range and median.
  • Modeling: Exponential growth was tested through a doubling-time scenario.

03. Strategic Recommendations

Reviewers of the April 2019 exam should prioritize experimental design vocabulary. Words like "random assignment," "bias," and "margin of error" must be understood deeply.

We also recommend practicing the extraction of information from complex tables where multiple categories are presented simultaneously.
