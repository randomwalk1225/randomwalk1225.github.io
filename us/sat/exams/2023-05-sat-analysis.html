May 2023 SAT Math Research Analysis

A detailed investigation into data analysis complexity, statistical inference, and computational precision trends in the May 2023 cohort.

Executive Summary

The May 2023 SAT administration was distinguished by a high-complexity Section 4 (Calculator), which focused heavily on sophisticated data interpretation and multi-variable statistical models. Unlike the March sitting, which leaned into abstract algebra, May tested students' endurance with reading-intensive problem solving.

2023 global data shows an average Math score of 520, with the May sitting highlighting a critical need for proficiency in identifying sampling bias and interpreting margin of error.

03. Scoring Strategy (Mark Scheme)

The May 2023 curve was less generous than the March 2023 curve for the mid-range scores (500-650) but remained fair for the upper percentiles. This was due to the "accessible" nature of the first half of Section 4, which was offset by the extreme difficulty of the final grid-in questions.

Strategic Note: In sittings like May 2023, maximizing points on the "Heart of Algebra" questions is critical, as the "Problem Solving" section has a higher probability of time-exhaustion.

04. Strategic Advice for Digital SAT Transition

The May 2023 experience offers several takeaways for the upcoming adaptive Digital SAT:

  • Stat Analysis Proficiency: The DSAT continues to emphasize statistical validity. The logic used in May 2023 Section 4 is a direct precursor to the "Inference" questions in the new format.
  • Efficiency over Brute Force: As demonstrated by the time-intensive Section 4, learning to use calculator shortcuts (and the built-in Desmos on DSAT) is no longer optional for top scores.
  • Concept Mastery: Transitioning from paper to digital means mastering Rational Function Transformations, which are frequently appearing as "filler" questions in Module 1 and "distractors" in Module 2.
