01. Conceptual Shifts: The Rise of Polynomial Remainder Theorem

The October 2022 SAT Math section continued a trend of including higher-order polynomial questions. Specifically, several problems required students to apply the **Remainder Theorem** (i.e., if \( p(a) = r \), then \( p(x)/(x-a) \) has remainder \( r \)) or identify factors through synthetic-style logic. This indicates a shift away from simple factoring towards abstract polynomial properties.

Students were also tested on their ability to work with linear systems containing variables as constants (e.g., \( ax + by = c \)), which requires a logical understanding of slopes and intercepts without numerical substitution.

02. Topic Distribution & Data Analysis Nuance

While the exam maintained the usual content balance, the **Problem Solving and Data Analysis** section focused heavily on conditional probability and restricted sample spaces.

  • Conditional Probability: Questions involving two-way tables required careful reading of the denominator. Many students faltered by using the total population instead of the restricted subgroup mentioned in the prompt.
  • Higher-Order Functions: We observed a higher density of rational functions, where students had to identify vertical asymptotes or domain restrictions.
  • Algebraic Fluency: Complex radicals and fractional exponents appeared in the multiple-choice section of Section 3, requiring mastery of the laws of exponents.

03. Strategic Recommendations

For students preparing based on this sitting, we recommend a deep dive into **higher-order polynomials and rational functions**. Understanding how to quickly manipulate standard forms into vertex or factored forms is crucial. For the statistics portion, practice "Of those who..." questions to master sample space restriction, as this is a frequent differentiator for high-scorers.
