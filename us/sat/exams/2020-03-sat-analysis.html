March 2020 SAT Math Research Analysis

An investigation into mastery of exponential and linear models, conceptual interpretation trends, and modeling dominance in the March 2020 administration.

Executive Summary

The March 2020 SAT administration continued the trend of valuing conceptual interpretation over raw calculation. Students were repeatedly asked to identify the meaning of specific constants and variables within exponential growth and linear contexts.

Research highlights that high-scoring students are increasingly challenged by the "bottleneck" of mapping mathematical symbols to real-world descriptors, particularly in Section 4's text-heavy data analysis.

01. Conceptual Shifts: Interpretation over Computation

The March 2020 exam continued the trend of valuing conceptual interpretation over raw calculation. Students were repeatedly asked to identify the meaning of specific constants and variables in context. For instance, in an exponential growth model \( P = 100(1.05)^t \), questions focused on what the 100 represented and how the 1.05 related to a percentage increase.

This shift requires students to slow down and map mathematical symbols to real-world descriptors, a skill that is increasingly becoming the bottleneck for computationally proficient students.

02. Topic Distribution & Challenging Areas

There was a high density of questions related to Scatterplots and Line of Best Fit. Students had to make predictions and interpret residuals conceptually.

Data Representation

Standard deviation appeared in a comparative format—students had to judge the spread based on frequency tables without manual calculation.

Algebraic Fluency

Systems of equations with complex coefficients were present in Section 3, testing manual manipulation skills under time pressure.

Unit Conversion Traps

Unit conversions were embedded within multi-step word problems, requiring careful reading to avoid "trap" answers based on inconsistent units.

03. Strategic Recommendations

Based on the March 2020 analysis, we recommend focusing on the following:

  • Dimensional Analysis: Practice unit consistency. Overlooking unit changes (e.g., minutes to hours) was a primary point of failure in this sitting.
  • Modeling Nuance: Mastery of linear vs. exponential differences (additive vs. multiplicative growth) remains essential for interpreting word problems.
  • Residual Logic: Understanding the conceptual meaning of "line of best fit" residuals and their implications for model accuracy.
