Top 10 Detailed Profiles

1 Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

MIT's Department of Mathematics consistently ranks #1 globally. With 60+ faculty members spanning every major area of mathematics, it produces groundbreaking research across pure and applied fields. The undergraduate math program (Course 18) is known for its rigour and flexibility.

Key facts: 60+ faculty · 6 Fields Medal affiliations · Undergraduate research through UROP · Located next to Harvard for cross-registration · World's top math library (Hayden).

2 Princeton University

Princeton's mathematics department is legendary, bolstered by its proximity to the Institute for Advanced Study (IAS), where Einstein and Gödel once worked. The department is strongest in pure mathematics — number theory, algebraic geometry, and analysis.

Key facts: IAS partnership · Intimate seminars · Certificate programme for non-majors · Beautiful Fine Hall library · Strong record of Fields Medal recipients.

3 Harvard University

Harvard's math department offers unmatched breadth, with world-class faculty in virtually every subfield. The Math 55 course sequence is famously regarded as the hardest undergraduate math course in the country.

Key facts: Math 55 (legendary course) · Cross-registration with MIT · Strong graduate programme · Extensive undergraduate research · Center of Mathematical Sciences and Applications (CMSA).

4 Stanford University

Stanford excels at the intersection of pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and computer science. Its Silicon Valley location means strong industry connections for students interested in math-adjacent careers in tech and finance.

Key facts: Strong math-CS overlap · Silicon Valley access · Undergraduate Honours programme · Active colloquium series · Interdisciplinary research emphasis.

5 UC Berkeley

The top public university for mathematics in the US. Berkeley's department is known for its strength in analysis, algebra, and mathematical logic. The campus culture encourages collaboration between departments.

Key facts: Top public university for math · MSRI (Mathematical Sciences Research Institute) nearby · Affordable relative to private peers · 50+ faculty · Strong PhD programme.

6 California Institute of Technology (Caltech)

Caltech is tiny (roughly 1,000 undergrads total) but offers an extraordinary student-to-faculty ratio. Every math student gets significant personal attention and research access from day one.

Key facts: ~3:1 student-faculty ratio · All students take rigorous math core · SURF research programme · Close collaboration with JPL · Intense, focused environment.

7 University of Chicago

Chicago's math programme is known for its pure mathematics strength and exceptionally rigorous curriculum. The first two years include the famous "Honors Calculus" sequence taught using Spivak's Calculus.

Key facts: Rigorous Spivak-based honours sequence · Strong algebra and geometry groups · REU programme · Intellectual, proof-focused culture · Chicago's economics connections.

8 Columbia University

Columbia's math department benefits from its New York City location, which enables partnerships with NYU Courant and access to finance industry research. Strong in probability, mathematical physics, and number theory.

Key facts: NYC location · Probability and math physics strength · Collaboration with NYU Courant · Active seminar programme · Summer undergraduate research.

9 Yale University

Yale's math department has grown significantly in recent years, with major faculty hires strengthening its research profile. The department is smaller and more personal than MIT or Harvard, which appeals to students wanting close mentorship.

Key facts: Growing department with major hires · Excellent undergraduate attention · Directed Studies program · Strong analysis and algebra groups · Generous financial aid.

10 UCLA

UCLA combines strong applied mathematics with excellent pure research. Its location in Los Angeles provides connections to the entertainment tech and aerospace industries, while the department's combinatorics and analysis groups are world-class.

Key facts: Top applied math programme · UCLA Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics (IPAM) · Strong combinatorics group · Affordable public tuition (CA residents) · 80+ faculty.

Public vs Private

Five of our top 20 are public universities (Berkeley, UCLA, Michigan, UT Austin, UW–Madison, Georgia Tech). Here's how they compare:

Important note on financial aid: Many top private universities (MIT, Princeton, Harvard, Stanford, Caltech, Yale) meet 100% of demonstrated financial need. After financial aid, they can cost less than public universities for low- and middle-income families. Always run the net price calculator before dismissing a school on price.

Tips for Applying to Top Math Programs

  1. Take challenging courses: AP Calculus BC, multivariable calculus, linear algebra, and any proof-based courses available.
  2. Math competitions: AMC 10/12, AIME, USAMO participation demonstrates mathematical talent beyond grades.
  3. Research experience: Summer research programmes (RSI, PROMYS, HCSSiM, SUMaC) are highly valued.
  4. Strong SAT/ACT math: A perfect or near-perfect math score is expected at the top 10.
  5. Letters of recommendation: A strong letter from a math teacher who can speak to your problem-solving ability matters more than generic praise.
  6. Genuine passion: Admissions committees can distinguish students who love math from those who merely perform well at it. Show independent exploration.
