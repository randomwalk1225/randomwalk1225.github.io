A New Integer Sequence in the OEIS
— The Journey of A392303

March 12, 2026
← Back to Blog

Sequence A392303, counting NE lattice paths under a discretized parabolic boundary, has been officially approved in the OEIS.

1. It Started with a Question

"What if we replace the diagonal with a parabola?"

One of the most celebrated numbers in combinatorics is the Catalan number $C_n$: the count of north-east (NE) lattice paths from $(0,0)$ to $(n,n)$ that stay weakly below the diagonal $y \leq x$. The Catalan numbers have a centuries-long history and a well-known closed form:

$$C_n = \frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}$$

But what happens if we replace the linear boundary with a parabolic one?

$$y \;\leq\; \left\lfloor \frac{x^2}{n} \right\rfloor$$

This article traces how that question became a research paper and, ultimately, a new entry in the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences.

2. Problem Definition

For each integer $n \geq 1$, we count NE lattice paths from $(0,0)$ to $(n,n)$ whose every vertex lies on or below the discretized parabolic boundary.

Definition. The discretized parabolic boundary: $$\hat{f}_n(x) = \left\lfloor \frac{x^2}{n} \right\rfloor, \quad 0 \leq x \leq n$$ Number of admissible paths: $p_n = N_n(n,n)$

Two features distinguish this from the classical Catalan setting:

  1. The boundary is curved — a parabola, not a line.
  2. The boundary depends on $n$ — changing the endpoint changes the constraint itself.

Because of the second property, standard fixed-boundary techniques such as the transfer-matrix method and the kernel method do not directly apply.

3. Main Results

Summary of Results
  • Fundamental recurrence: $N_n(x,y) = N_n(x-1,y) + N_n(x,y-1)$ with boundary conditions
  • Efficient computation: $O(n^2)$ time, $O(n)$ space via column-by-column DP
  • Catalan comparison: $1 \leq p_n < C_n$ for all $n \geq 2$
  • P-recursive search: No P-recursive relation of order $\leq 6$, degree $\leq 5$ found in the first 500 terms

4. The OEIS Registration Process

March 9, 2026 — Submission

The core sequence was submitted to the OEIS with 10 initial terms, a Python implementation, and a mathematical description.

March 10, 2026 — Editor Review

Within a day, OEIS editor Hugo Pfoertner began the review. Alois P. Heinz wrote a Maple program to independently verify and massively extend the sequence.

March 10, 2026 — Approved

After review and cross-verification, the sequence was officially approved as A392303.

A392303: Number of NE lattice paths from (0,0) to (n,n) staying weakly below the discretized parabolic boundary $y = \lfloor x^2/n \rfloor$.

6. Open Problems

Several natural questions remain unanswered:

  • Asymptotics: What is the growth rate of $p_n$?   $p_n \sim\; ?$   as $n \to \infty$
  • Closed form: Does a neat formula exist, analogous to the Catalan formula?
  • D-finiteness: Is the generating function D-finite?
  • Probabilistic interpretation: Is there a connection to discrete Brownian bridges?

Resolving any one of these would be a meaningful contribution in its own right.

7. Reflections

Registering a new sequence in the OEIS as an independent researcher was a different experience from submitting a journal paper. Within a single day, expert editors verified the code and extended the sequence to 1,830 terms — a testament to the remarkable capability and dedication of the OEIS community.

Having a piece of mathematical research permanently recorded in a public database is, in itself, one of the most rewarding outcomes of this work.
