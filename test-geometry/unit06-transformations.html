Unit 6 — Transformations and Congruence

Grade 9–10 · Geometry · Topics: Translations, Reflections, Rotations, Compositions, Congruence

6.1 Introduction to Transformations

Definition — Transformation

A transformation is a function that maps each point of a figure (the pre-image) to a new point in the plane (the image).

A rigid motion (or isometry) is a transformation that preserves both size and shape — that is, side lengths and angle measures are unchanged. The three basic rigid motions are translations, reflections, and rotations.

Notation: if point $A$ maps to its image, we write $A \to A'$ (read "$A$ prime"). For a triangle: $\triangle ABC \to \triangle A'B'C'$.

6.2 Translations

Definition — Translation

A translation slides every point of a figure the same distance in the same direction. The direction and distance are described by a translation vector $\langle a,\, b \rangle$.

Coordinate rule:

$$(x,\, y) \;\to\; (x + a,\; y + b)$$

Example 6.2 — Applying a Translation

Translate $\triangle ABC$ with $A(1,2)$, $B(3,4)$, $C(5,1)$ by vector $\langle -2,\, 3 \rangle$.

Rule: $(x,y) \to (x - 2,\; y + 3)$

  • $A(1,2) \to A'(1-2,\; 2+3) = A'(-1, 5)$
  • $B(3,4) \to B'(3-2,\; 4+3) = B'(1, 7)$
  • $C(5,1) \to C'(5-2,\; 1+3) = C'(3, 4)$

6.3 Reflections

Definition — Reflection

A reflection flips a figure over a line called the line of reflection. Each point maps to a point on the opposite side of the line, the same perpendicular distance from it.

Line of Reflection Coordinate Rule
$x$-axis$(x,y) \to (x,\,-y)$
$y$-axis$(x,y) \to (-x,\,y)$
$y = x$$(x,y) \to (y,\,x)$
$y = -x$$(x,y) \to (-y,\,-x)$

Example 6.3 — Applying Reflections

Reflect $A(3, -2)$ over the $x$-axis, then over the $y$-axis.

  • Over $x$-axis: $(x,y) \to (x,-y)$, so $A(3,-2) \to A'(3, 2)$.
  • Over $y$-axis: $(x,y) \to (-x,y)$, so $A(3,-2) \to A'(-3, -2)$.

6.4 Rotations

Definition — Rotation

A rotation turns a figure around a fixed point called the center of rotation by a given angle. Angles are measured counterclockwise (CCW) unless stated otherwise.

Coordinate rules about the origin:

Rotation Rule
90° CCW$(x,y) \to (-y,\,x)$
180°$(x,y) \to (-x,\,-y)$
270° CCW (= 90° CW)$(x,y) \to (y,\,-x)$
360°$(x,y) \to (x,\,y)$ (identity)

Example 6.4 — Applying a Rotation

Rotate $B(4, 1)$ by $90°$ counterclockwise about the origin.

Rule for 90° CCW: $(x, y) \to (-y, x)$

$$B(4, 1) \to B'(-1, 4)$$

6.5 Compositions of Transformations

Definition — Composition and Glide Reflection

A composition of transformations applies two or more transformations in sequence. The output of the first becomes the input of the second.

A glide reflection is the composition of a translation followed by a reflection over a line parallel to the translation direction.

Key Composition Results

  • Two reflections over parallel lines → equivalent to a translation (distance = twice the distance between the lines, direction perpendicular to them).
  • Two reflections over intersecting lines → equivalent to a rotation (angle = twice the angle between the lines, center at the intersection point).

6.6 Congruence via Transformations

Theorem — Congruence and Rigid Motions

Two figures are congruent if and only if one can be mapped to the other by a sequence of rigid motions (translations, reflections, and/or rotations).

This gives a precise meaning to congruence: same shape and same size, in any orientation or position.

Example 6.6 — Describing a Rigid Motion

$\triangle ABC$ has vertices $A(0,0)$, $B(4,0)$, $C(2,3)$. $\triangle DEF$ has vertices $D(0,0)$, $E(-4,0)$, $F(-2,3)$.

Describe the rigid motion.

Notice each $x$-coordinate negates while $y$ stays the same: $(x,y) \to (-x,y)$. This is a reflection over the $y$-axis. Therefore $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$.

Practice Problems

Unit 6 Practice — 10 Problems

  1. Translate $P(3, -5)$ by vector $\langle 4, -2 \rangle$. Give the coordinates of $P'$.
  2. Translate $\triangle ABC$ with $A(-1,0)$, $B(2,1)$, $C(0,3)$ by $\langle 3, -4 \rangle$. List all image coordinates.
  3. Reflect $Q(5, 2)$ over the $x$-axis.
  4. Reflect $R(-3, 4)$ over the line $y = x$.
  5. Reflect $S(6, -1)$ over the $y$-axis, then over the $x$-axis. What single transformation is equivalent?
  6. Rotate $T(0, 5)$ by $90°$ CCW about the origin.
  7. Rotate $U(-2, 3)$ by $180°$ about the origin.
  8. Rotate $V(4, -4)$ by $270°$ CCW about the origin.
  9. A figure is reflected over the line $x = 1$, then over the line $x = 5$. Describe the equivalent single transformation.
  10. Are two figures that are related by a rotation congruent? Justify your answer.
