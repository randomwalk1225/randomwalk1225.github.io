Unit 7 — Similarity

Grade 9–10 · Geometry · Topics: Ratios, Similar Polygons, Dilations, Similarity Theorems, Proportionality

7.1 Ratios and Proportions

Definition — Ratio and Proportion

A ratio is a comparison of two quantities by division, written as $\dfrac{a}{b}$ or $a : b$ (where $b \ne 0$).

A proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal:

$$\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$$

Cross-multiplication property: If $\dfrac{a}{b} = \dfrac{c}{d}$, then $ad = bc$. This is the standard method for solving proportions.

Example 7.1 — Solving a Proportion

Solve: $\dfrac{x}{5} = \dfrac{12}{20}$

Solution: Cross-multiply:

$$20x = 5 \cdot 12 = 60$$ $$x = \frac{60}{20} = \boxed{3}$$

7.2 Similar Polygons

Definition — Similar Polygons

Two polygons are similar (symbol: $\sim$) if and only if:

  1. All pairs of corresponding angles are congruent, AND
  2. All pairs of corresponding sides are proportional.

We write $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$ to indicate the correspondence $A \leftrightarrow D$, $B \leftrightarrow E$, $C \leftrightarrow F$.

Definition — Scale Factor

The scale factor of two similar figures is the ratio of any pair of corresponding side lengths:

$$k = \frac{\text{side of image}}{\text{corresponding side of pre-image}}$$

Theorem — Perimeters and Areas of Similar Figures

If two similar figures have scale factor $k$, then:

  • Their perimeters have ratio $k : 1$ (linear scale).
  • Their areas have ratio $k^2 : 1$ (square of the scale factor).

Example 7.2 — Using Scale Factor for Perimeter

$\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$ with scale factor $3 : 2$. If the perimeter of $\triangle ABC$ is 30, find the perimeter of $\triangle DEF$.

Solution: Perimeters share the same ratio as the scale factor:

$$\frac{\text{Perimeter of } \triangle ABC}{\text{Perimeter of } \triangle DEF} = \frac{3}{2}$$ $$\frac{30}{\text{Perimeter of } \triangle DEF} = \frac{3}{2}$$ $$\text{Perimeter of } \triangle DEF = \frac{30 \times 2}{3} = \boxed{20}$$

7.3 Dilations

Definition — Dilation

A dilation with center $O$ and scale factor $k$ maps every point $P$ to a point $P'$ such that $OP' = k \cdot OP$, with $P'$ on ray $\overrightarrow{OP}$.

Coordinate rule about the origin:

$$(x,\, y) \;\to\; (kx,\; ky)$$
  • $k > 1$: enlargement (image is larger).
  • $0 < k < 1$: reduction (image is smaller).
  • $k = 1$: identity (no change).

A dilation is not a rigid motion (unless $k = 1$), but it does preserve angle measures and produces a similar figure.

Example 7.3 — Applying a Dilation

Dilate $A(2, 3)$ by scale factor $k = \dfrac{1}{2}$ about the origin.

Rule: $(x,y) \to \left(\tfrac{1}{2}x,\; \tfrac{1}{2}y\right)$

$$A(2,3) \;\to\; A'\!\left(1,\; 1.5\right)$$

The image is a reduction — it is half the distance from the origin.

7.4 Proving Triangles Similar

Theorem — AA Similarity (Angle-Angle)

If two angles of one triangle are congruent to two angles of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.

Since the angle sum is always $180°$, congruent pairs of two angles guarantee the third angles are also congruent.

Theorem — SSS Similarity (Side-Side-Side)

If all three pairs of corresponding sides of two triangles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.

$$\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} = \frac{CA}{FD} \implies \triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$$

Theorem — SAS Similarity (Side-Angle-Side)

If two pairs of corresponding sides are proportional and the included angles are congruent, then the triangles are similar.

$$\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{AC}{DF} \text{ and } \angle A \cong \angle D \implies \triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$$

Common Mistake — SSA Does NOT Work

SSA (two sides proportional + a non-included angle congruent) does NOT guarantee similarity. Just as SSA fails for triangle congruence, it also fails for similarity. Always verify you have AA, SSS, or SAS before concluding two triangles are similar.

7.5 Using Similar Triangles

Example 7.5a — Indirect Measurement (Shadow Method)

A 6-foot pole casts a 4-foot shadow at the same time that a tree casts a 20-foot shadow. Find the height $h$ of the tree.

Setup: The pole and its shadow form a triangle similar to the tree and its shadow (same sun angle → AA).

$$\frac{6}{4} = \frac{h}{20}$$ $$4h = 120 \implies h = \boxed{30 \text{ ft}}$$

Example 7.5b — Parallel Line Inside a Triangle

In $\triangle ABC$, $\overline{DE} \parallel \overline{BC}$ with $D$ on $\overline{AB}$ and $E$ on $\overline{AC}$. Given $AD = 3$, $DB = 5$, $AE = 4$, find $EC$.

Solution: By the Triangle Proportionality Theorem:

$$\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}$$ $$\frac{3}{5} = \frac{4}{EC}$$ $$EC = \frac{4 \times 5}{3} = \boxed{\frac{20}{3}}$$

7.6 Triangle Proportionality Theorem

Theorem — Triangle Proportionality Theorem

If a line is parallel to one side of a triangle and intersects the other two sides, then it divides those two sides proportionally.

If $\overline{DE} \parallel \overline{BC}$ in $\triangle ABC$ (with $D$ on $\overline{AB}$, $E$ on $\overline{AC}$), then:

$$\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}$$

Theorem — Converse of Triangle Proportionality Theorem

If a line divides two sides of a triangle proportionally, then it is parallel to the third side.

If $\dfrac{AD}{DB} = \dfrac{AE}{EC}$, then $\overline{DE} \parallel \overline{BC}$.

Tip — Whole-Side vs. Partial-Side Ratios

The Proportionality Theorem uses partial sides (e.g., $AD : DB$). You can also set up a proportion using whole sides:

$$\frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{AE}{AC}$$

Both forms are equivalent. Use whichever is more convenient for the given information.

Practice Problems

Unit 7 Practice — 12 Problems

  1. Solve the proportion: $\dfrac{3}{x} = \dfrac{9}{15}$.
  2. Solve the proportion: $\dfrac{x+1}{4} = \dfrac{6}{8}$.
  3. $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle XYZ$ with $AB = 6$, $XY = 9$. Find the scale factor from $\triangle ABC$ to $\triangle XYZ$.
  4. Two similar rectangles have scale factor $5:2$. If the area of the larger rectangle is 125 cm², find the area of the smaller one.
  5. Dilate $B(6, -4)$ by scale factor $k = 3$ about the origin. Give the image coordinates.
  6. Dilate $C(-8, 4)$ by scale factor $k = \frac{1}{4}$ about the origin. Give the image coordinates.
  7. Are $\triangle PQR$ and $\triangle STU$ similar if $\angle P = 50°$, $\angle Q = 70°$, $\angle S = 50°$, $\angle T = 70°$? Which theorem applies?
  8. $\triangle ABC$ has sides 4, 6, 8. $\triangle DEF$ has sides 6, 9, 12. Are they similar? Which theorem applies?
  9. A flagpole casts a 15-ft shadow. At the same time, a 5-ft person casts a 3-ft shadow. Find the height of the flagpole.
  10. In $\triangle MNP$, line $\overline{QR} \parallel \overline{NP}$ with $Q$ on $\overline{MN}$, $R$ on $\overline{MP}$. If $MQ = 4$, $QN = 6$, $MR = 5$, find $RP$.
  11. In $\triangle ABC$, $D$ is on $\overline{AB}$ and $E$ is on $\overline{AC}$ with $AD = 5$, $AB = 15$, $AE = 4$, $AC = 12$. Is $\overline{DE} \parallel \overline{BC}$? Explain.
  12. $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$ with scale factor $2:5$. If the perimeter of $\triangle DEF$ is 40, find the perimeter of $\triangle ABC$.
