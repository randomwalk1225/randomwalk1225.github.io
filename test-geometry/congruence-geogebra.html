Triangle Congruence: SSS, SAS, and ASA

Geometry • High School • MathHub Global • February 2026

Two triangles are congruent when they have exactly the same shape and size ‚Äî one can be placed perfectly on top of the other. Use the interactive applets below to drag vertices and explore how each congruence condition works.

What Does Congruence Mean?

Definition ‚Äî Congruent Triangles

$\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$ means all six corresponding parts match:

  • Three pairs of congruent sides: $AB=DE,\; BC=EF,\; CA=FD$
  • Three pairs of congruent angles: $\angle A=\angle D,\; \angle B=\angle E,\; \angle C=\angle F$

Just three carefully chosen parts are enough ‚Äî that is what SSS, SAS, and ASA tell us.

1. SSS ‚Äî Side-Side-Side

SSS Congruence Postulate

If three sides of one triangle are congruent to three sides of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.

$$\overline{AB}\cong\overline{DE},\;\overline{BC}\cong\overline{EF},\;\overline{CA}\cong\overline{FD} \;\Longrightarrow\; \triangle ABC\cong\triangle DEF$$

üîµ SSS Explorer ‚Äî Drag the vertices of ‚ñ≥ABC

Drag A, B, or C to change the triangle. The side lengths a, b, c update in real time. Any other triangle with the same three side lengths will be congruent to this one.

üí° Why SSS Works

A triangle is a rigid frame. Once three side lengths are fixed, there is only one possible shape ‚Äî the frame cannot flex. That rigidity makes SSS a valid proof method.

Example 1 ‚Äî SSS

$AB=5,\;BC=7,\;CA=6,\;DE=5,\;EF=7,\;FD=6.$

  1. $\overline{AB}\cong\overline{DE}$ ‚úì
  2. $\overline{BC}\cong\overline{EF}$ ‚úì
  3. $\overline{CA}\cong\overline{FD}$ ‚úì
  4. $\therefore\;\triangle ABC\cong\triangle DEF$ by SSS

2. SAS ‚Äî Side-Angle-Side

SAS Congruence Postulate

If two sides and the included angle between them of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding parts of another, the triangles are congruent.

$$\overline{AB}\cong\overline{DE},\;\angle B\cong\angle E,\;\overline{BC}\cong\overline{EF} \;\Longrightarrow\; \triangle ABC\cong\triangle DEF$$

üü° SAS Explorer ‚Äî Adjust sides AB, BC and included angle B

Move the sliders to change the two side lengths and the angle between them. Once those three values are set, there is exactly one possible triangle shape.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Included Angle Matters

SSA is NOT a valid congruence rule. The angle must be between the two sides ‚Äî not on the outside. SSA can produce two different triangles from the same measurements.

Example 2 ‚Äî SAS

$\overline{KL}\cong\overline{NP},\;\overline{LM}\cong\overline{PQ},\;\angle L\cong\angle P.$

  1. $\overline{KL}\cong\overline{NP}$ ‚úì
  2. $\angle L\cong\angle P$ (included angle) ‚úì
  3. $\overline{LM}\cong\overline{PQ}$ ‚úì
  4. $\therefore\;\triangle KLM\cong\triangle NPQ$ by SAS

3. ASA ‚Äî Angle-Side-Angle

ASA Congruence Postulate

If two angles and the included side between them of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding parts of another, the triangles are congruent.

$$\angle A\cong\angle D,\;\overline{AB}\cong\overline{DE},\;\angle B\cong\angle E \;\Longrightarrow\; \triangle ABC\cong\triangle DEF$$

üü¢ ASA Explorer ‚Äî Adjust angles A, B and the included side AB

Move the sliders to change ‚à†A, ‚à†B, and the length of AB. Notice: once two angles and the included side are fixed, the third vertex C is completely determined.

üí° ASA vs AAS

In ASA, the side is between the two angles. In AAS, it is not. Both are valid, but name them correctly in proofs.

Example 3 ‚Äî ASA

$\angle R\cong\angle U,\;\overline{RS}\cong\overline{UV},\;\angle S\cong\angle V.$

  1. $\angle R\cong\angle U$ ‚úì
  2. $\overline{RS}\cong\overline{UV}$ (included side) ‚úì
  3. $\angle S\cong\angle V$ ‚úì
  4. $\therefore\;\triangle RST\cong\triangle UVW$ by ASA

Practice Problems

Problem 1

$\overline{MN}\cong\overline{QR}$, $\overline{NP}\cong\overline{RS}$, $\overline{MP}\cong\overline{QS}$. Which postulate proves $\triangle MNP\cong\triangle QRS$?

Show Answer

SSS ‚Äî all three side pairs are congruent.

Problem 2

$\angle X\cong\angle A$, $\overline{XY}\cong\overline{AB}$, $\angle Y\cong\angle B$. Which postulate?

Show Answer

ASA ‚Äî two angles with the included side $\overline{XY}$.

Problem 3

$\overline{JK}\cong\overline{PQ}$, $\overline{KL}\cong\overline{QR}$, $\angle K\cong\angle Q$. Which postulate?

Show Answer

SAS ‚Äî two sides with the included angle $\angle K$.
