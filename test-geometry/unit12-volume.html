Unit 12 — Surface Area and Volume

Grade 9–10 · Geometry · MathHub

Surface Area Formula Reference

$B$ = area of base, $P$ = perimeter of base, $h$ = height, $l$ = slant height, $r$ = radius.

Shape Lateral Area Total Surface Area
Prism$Ph$$2B + Ph$
Cylinder$2\pi rh$$2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh$
Pyramid$\dfrac{1}{2}Pl$$B + \dfrac{1}{2}Pl$
Cone$\pi rl$$\pi r^2 + \pi rl$
Sphere—$4\pi r^2$

Shape Volume
Prism$V = Bh$
Cylinder$V = \pi r^2 h$
Pyramid$V = \dfrac{1}{3}Bh$
Cone$V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
Sphere$V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$

12.1 Three-Dimensional Figures

Definition — 3D Figures and Key Vocabulary

  • Polyhedron: A 3D figure with flat polygonal faces. Examples: prisms and pyramids.
  • Prism: Two congruent parallel bases connected by rectangular lateral faces.
  • Pyramid: One polygonal base with triangular lateral faces meeting at an apex.
  • Cylinder, Cone, Sphere: Non-polyhedra with curved surfaces.
  • Net: A 2D flat pattern that folds into a 3D figure — useful for computing surface area.
  • Cross-section: The 2D shape formed by intersecting a solid with a plane.
  • Euler's Formula (for polyhedra): $V - E + F = 2$, where $V$ = vertices, $E$ = edges, $F$ = faces.

12.2 Surface Area of Prisms and Cylinders

Example — Rectangular Prism

A rectangular prism with dimensions $3 \times 4 \times 5$.

There are three pairs of faces: $3\times4$, $3\times5$, and $4\times5$.

$SA = 2(lw + lh + wh) = 2(12 + 15 + 20) = 2(47) = \mathbf{94}$ sq units

Example — Cylinder

A cylinder with radius $r = 3$ and height $h = 8$.

$SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh = 2\pi(9) + 2\pi(3)(8) = 18\pi + 48\pi = 66\pi \approx \mathbf{207.3}$ sq units

12.3 Surface Area of Pyramids and Cones

Example — Square Pyramid

A square pyramid with base $6 \times 6$ and slant height $l = 5$.

Base area $B = 36$. Perimeter of base $P = 24$.

$SA = B + \dfrac{1}{2}Pl = 36 + \dfrac{1}{2}(24)(5) = 36 + 60 = \mathbf{96}$ sq units

Example — Cone

A cone with radius $r = 4$ and slant height $l = 9$.

$SA = \pi r^2 + \pi rl = \pi(16) + \pi(4)(9) = 16\pi + 36\pi = 52\pi \approx \mathbf{163.4}$ sq units

12.4 Volume of Prisms and Cylinders

Cavalieri's Principle

If two solids have the same height and the same cross-sectional area at every level, then they have equal volumes. This principle justifies applying the same volume formula to oblique (slanted) and right prisms/cylinders.

Example — Triangular Prism

A triangular prism with triangular base having legs $6$ and $8$, and height (length) $h = 10$.

Base area $B = \dfrac{1}{2}(6)(8) = 24$

$V = Bh = 24 \times 10 = \mathbf{240}$ cubic units

Example — Cylinder

A cylinder with radius $r = 5$ and height $h = 12$.

$V = \pi r^2 h = \pi(25)(12) = 300\pi \approx \mathbf{942.5}$ cubic units

12.5 Volume of Pyramids and Cones

Example — Square Pyramid

A square pyramid with $6 \times 6$ base and height $h = 9$.

Base area $B = 36$.

$V = \dfrac{1}{3}Bh = \dfrac{1}{3}(36)(9) = \mathbf{108}$ cubic units

Example — Cone

A cone with radius $r = 3$ and height $h = 7$.

$V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi(9)(7) = \dfrac{63\pi}{3} = 21\pi \approx \mathbf{66.0}$ cubic units

12.6 Spheres

Example — Sphere Surface Area and Volume

A sphere with radius $r = 6$.

Surface Area: $SA = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi(36) = 144\pi \approx \mathbf{452.4}$ sq units

Volume: $V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi(216) = 288\pi \approx \mathbf{904.8}$ cubic units

12.7 Similar Solids

Theorem — Similar Solids and Scale Factor

If two similar solids have a scale factor of $k : 1$ (or $k$), then:

  • Corresponding lengths: ratio $= k$
  • Corresponding areas (and surface areas): ratio $= k^2$
  • Corresponding volumes: ratio $= k^3$

This applies to all linear measurements (edge lengths, perimeters, slant heights) and their associated areas and volumes.

Example — Similar Prisms

Two similar prisms have scale factor $2 : 3$. The surface area of the smaller prism is $40$ square units. Find the surface area of the larger prism.

SA ratio $= \left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{9}{4}$

$SA_{\text{large}} = 40 \times \dfrac{9}{4} = \mathbf{90}$ square units

Practice Problems

Unit 12 Practice — 12 Problems

  1. A rectangular prism has dimensions $2 \times 5 \times 8$. Find its surface area and volume.
  2. A cylinder has radius $4$ and height $10$. Find the lateral area and total surface area.
  3. Find the volume of the cylinder in Problem 2.
  4. A triangular prism has an equilateral triangle base with side $6$ (area $= 9\sqrt{3}$) and height $15$. Find the volume.
  5. A square pyramid has base side $8$ and slant height $10$. Find the total surface area.
  6. A cone has radius $5$ and slant height $13$. Find the total surface area in terms of $\pi$.
  7. A cone has radius $6$ and height $8$. Find the volume.
  8. A square pyramid has base $9 \times 9$ and height $12$. Find the volume.
  9. A sphere has diameter $10$. Find the surface area and volume.
  10. A polyhedron has $12$ vertices and $18$ edges. Use Euler's formula to find the number of faces.
  11. Two similar pyramids have scale factor $3 : 5$. If the volume of the smaller pyramid is $54$, find the volume of the larger pyramid.
  12. Two similar cones have surface areas of $36\pi$ and $100\pi$. What is the ratio of their volumes?
