Unit 13 — Coordinate Geometry

Grade 9–10 · Geometry · MathHub

13.1 The Distance Formula

Theorem — Distance Formula

The distance between two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ in the coordinate plane is:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

Derivation: Draw a right triangle with the segment as the hypotenuse. The horizontal leg has length $|x_2 - x_1|$ and the vertical leg has length $|y_2 - y_1|$. Applying the Pythagorean Theorem gives the distance formula.

Distance Formula Diagram

Example — Distance Between Two Points

Find the distance between $A(-1, 3)$ and $B(5, -5)$.

$d = \sqrt{(5-(-1))^2 + (-5-3)^2}$

$d = \sqrt{6^2 + (-8)^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = \mathbf{10}$

13.2 The Midpoint Formula

Theorem — Midpoint Formula

The midpoint $M$ of the segment connecting $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is:

$$M = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\; \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$$

The midpoint is the average of the $x$-coordinates and the average of the $y$-coordinates.

Example 1 — Finding the Midpoint

Find the midpoint of $A(2, -4)$ and $B(8, 6)$.

$M = \left(\dfrac{2+8}{2},\; \dfrac{-4+6}{2}\right) = \left(\dfrac{10}{2},\; \dfrac{2}{2}\right) = \mathbf{(5, 1)}$

Example 2 — Finding an Endpoint from the Midpoint

$M(3, 7)$ is the midpoint of $\overline{AB}$ where $A = (1, 5)$. Find $B$.

$3 = \dfrac{1 + x_B}{2} \Rightarrow 6 = 1 + x_B \Rightarrow x_B = 5$

$7 = \dfrac{5 + y_B}{2} \Rightarrow 14 = 5 + y_B \Rightarrow y_B = 9$

$B = \mathbf{(5, 9)}$

13.3 Slope

Theorem — Slope of a Line

The slope of the line through $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is:

$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}$$
  • Horizontal line: slope $= 0$
  • Vertical line: slope $=$ undefined

Theorem — Parallel and Perpendicular Slopes

  • Parallel lines have equal slopes: $m_1 = m_2$.
  • Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals: $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$, i.e., $m_2 = -\dfrac{1}{m_1}$.

Example — Parallel Lines

Are $AB$ and $CD$ parallel? $A(0,0)$, $B(3,6)$, $C(1,2)$, $D(4,8)$.

Slope of $AB = \dfrac{6-0}{3-0} = 2$

Slope of $CD = \dfrac{8-2}{4-1} = \dfrac{6}{3} = 2$

Since the slopes are equal, $AB \parallel CD$. Yes, they are parallel.

13.4 Coordinate Proofs

Strategy — Setting Up Coordinate Proofs

Place geometric figures strategically on the coordinate plane to simplify calculations:

  • Put a right angle at the origin and one side along the positive $x$-axis.
  • Use variables like $a$, $b$, $c$ for general side lengths to keep the proof general.
  • Center symmetric figures at the origin (coordinates come in $\pm$ pairs).
  • After placing the figure, use the distance, midpoint, or slope formula to prove the property.

Example — Diagonals of a Rectangle Bisect Each Other

Prove: The diagonals of a rectangle bisect each other.

Setup: Place rectangle $OABC$ with $O(0,0)$, $A(a,0)$, $B(a,b)$, $C(0,b)$.

Midpoint of diagonal $\overline{OB}$: from $(0,0)$ to $(a,b)$.

$M_1 = \left(\dfrac{0+a}{2},\; \dfrac{0+b}{2}\right) = \left(\dfrac{a}{2},\; \dfrac{b}{2}\right)$

Midpoint of diagonal $\overline{AC}$: from $(a,0)$ to $(0,b)$.

$M_2 = \left(\dfrac{a+0}{2},\; \dfrac{0+b}{2}\right) = \left(\dfrac{a}{2},\; \dfrac{b}{2}\right)$

$M_1 = M_2$, so both diagonals share the same midpoint. Therefore the diagonals bisect each other. $\blacksquare$

13.5 Equations of Circles

Standard Form — Equation of a Circle

A circle with center $(h, k)$ and radius $r$:

$$(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$$

Expanding gives the general form: $x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$. To convert back to standard form, complete the square in both $x$ and $y$.

Example 1 — Equation from Center and a Point on the Circle

Find the equation of the circle with center $(-2, 4)$ passing through $(1, 4)$.

$r = \sqrt{(1-(-2))^2 + (4-4)^2} = \sqrt{9 + 0} = 3$

$(x - (-2))^2 + (y - 4)^2 = 3^2$

$$\boxed{(x+2)^2 + (y-4)^2 = 9}$$

Example 2 — Completing the Square

Find the center and radius: $x^2 + y^2 + 4x - 8y + 4 = 0$.

Rearrange and complete the square:

$(x^2 + 4x + 4) + (y^2 - 8y + 16) = -4 + 4 + 16$

$(x + 2)^2 + (y - 4)^2 = 16$

Center: $(-2,\; 4)$, Radius: $r = \sqrt{16} = 4$

Practice Problems

Unit 13 Practice — 12 Problems

  1. Find the distance between $P(3, 1)$ and $Q(7, 4)$.
  2. Find the distance between $M(-2, -3)$ and $N(4, 5)$. Round to the nearest tenth.
  3. Find the midpoint of $A(-4, 6)$ and $B(10, -2)$.
  4. The midpoint of $\overline{CD}$ is $(2, -1)$ and $C = (5, 3)$. Find $D$.
  5. Find the slope of the line through $(-3, 7)$ and $(5, -1)$.
  6. Line $\ell_1$ has slope $\dfrac{3}{4}$. Line $\ell_2$ is perpendicular to $\ell_1$. Find the slope of $\ell_2$.
  7. Show that the quadrilateral with vertices $A(0,0)$, $B(4,0)$, $C(5,3)$, $D(1,3)$ is a parallelogram using slopes.
  8. Show that $A(1,1)$, $B(4,1)$, $C(4,5)$ form a right triangle using slopes, then find the hypotenuse length.
  9. A segment has midpoint $(0, 0)$ and one endpoint $(-3, 5)$. Find the other endpoint.
  10. Write the equation of the circle with center $(5, -3)$ and radius $7$.
  11. Find the center and radius: $x^2 + y^2 - 10x + 6y + 9 = 0$.
  12. Two points on a circle are $A(1, 7)$ and $B(7, 7)$. If the center lies on the $x$-axis, find the center and radius.
