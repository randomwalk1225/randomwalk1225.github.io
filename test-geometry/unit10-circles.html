Unit 10 — Circles

Grade 9–10 · Geometry · MathHub

10.1 Parts of a Circle

Definition — Circle and Its Parts

A circle is the set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point called the center.

  • Radius ($r$): a segment from the center to any point on the circle.
  • Diameter ($d$): a chord passing through the center; $d = 2r$.
  • Chord: a segment whose endpoints are both on the circle.
  • Secant: a line that intersects a circle at exactly two points.
  • Tangent: a line that intersects a circle at exactly one point (the point of tangency).

10.2 Properties of Tangents

Theorem — Tangent–Radius Perpendicularity

A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius drawn to the point of tangency.

If line $t$ is tangent to circle $O$ at point $P$, then $OP \perp t$.

Theorem — Two Tangents from an External Point

If two tangent segments are drawn from the same external point to a circle, then the two tangent segments are congruent.

If $PA$ and $PB$ are tangent to circle $O$, then $PA = PB$.

Example — Tangent Segments

$PA$ and $PB$ are tangents from external point $P$ to circle $O$. $PA = 3x - 4$ and $PB = x + 8$. Find $PA$.

Since both are tangent segments from the same point: $PA = PB$

$3x - 4 = x + 8$

$2x = 12 \Rightarrow x = 6$

$PA = 3(6) - 4 = \mathbf{14}$

10.3 Arcs and Central Angles

Definition — Arcs and Central Angles

A central angle is an angle whose vertex is at the center of the circle. Its measure equals the measure of its intercepted arc.

  • Minor arc: measure less than $180°$ (named with two points, e.g., $\overset{\frown}{AB}$).
  • Major arc: measure greater than $180°$ (named with three points, e.g., $\overset{\frown}{ACB}$).
  • Semicircle: measure exactly $180°$ (half the circle).

Arc Addition Postulate

If point $C$ is on $\overset{\frown}{AB}$, then $m\overset{\frown}{AC} + m\overset{\frown}{CB} = m\overset{\frown}{AB}$.

Also: $m(\text{minor arc}) + m(\text{major arc}) = 360°$.

Example — Central Angle and Arc

Central angle $\angle AOB = 75°$. Find arc $AB$ and the major arc.

$m\overset{\frown}{AB} = 75°$ (arc = central angle)

Major arc $= 360° - 75° = \mathbf{285°}$

10.4 Chords and Arcs

Theorem — Congruent Chords and Arcs

In the same circle (or congruent circles):

  • Congruent chords intercept congruent arcs.
  • Congruent arcs are intercepted by congruent chords.

Theorem — Perpendicular from Center to Chord

If a diameter (or radius) is perpendicular to a chord, then it bisects the chord and its arc.

Conversely, the perpendicular bisector of a chord passes through the center of the circle.

10.5 Inscribed Angles

Inscribed Angle Theorem

An inscribed angle is an angle whose vertex is on the circle and whose sides are chords. Its measure is half the intercepted arc:

$$\angle \text{inscribed} = \frac{1}{2} \times m\overset{\frown}{\text{arc}}$$

Corollaries of the Inscribed Angle Theorem

  • Same arc: Inscribed angles that intercept the same arc are congruent.
  • Semicircle: An inscribed angle that intercepts a semicircle (diameter) measures exactly $90°$.
  • Inscribed quadrilateral: Opposite angles of a quadrilateral inscribed in a circle are supplementary (sum to $180°$).

Example — Inscribed Angle

An inscribed angle measures $35°$. Find the intercepted arc.

$\text{arc} = 2 \times 35° = \mathbf{70°}$

10.6 Other Angle Relationships in Circles

Theorem — Angle Formed by Two Chords Inside a Circle

When two chords intersect inside a circle, the measure of the angle formed equals half the sum of the intercepted arcs:

$$\angle = \frac{1}{2}(m\overset{\frown}{\text{arc}_1} + m\overset{\frown}{\text{arc}_2})$$

Theorem — Angle Formed by Secants/Tangents Outside a Circle

When two secants, two tangents, or a secant and a tangent meet at a point outside the circle, the angle equals half the (positive) difference of the intercepted arcs:

$$\angle = \frac{1}{2}\left|m\overset{\frown}{\text{far arc}} - m\overset{\frown}{\text{near arc}}\right|$$

10.7 Segment Length Relationships

Theorem — Two Chords Intersecting Inside

If two chords intersect at a point inside the circle, the products of their segments are equal:

$$a \cdot b = c \cdot d$$

where $a, b$ are segments of one chord and $c, d$ are segments of the other.

Theorem — Two Secants from an External Point

If two secants are drawn from an external point:

$$(\text{whole}_1)(\text{external}_1) = (\text{whole}_2)(\text{external}_2)$$

Theorem — Secant and Tangent from an External Point

If a secant and a tangent are drawn from an external point:

$$(\text{whole secant})(\text{external part}) = (\text{tangent})^2$$

10.8 Equation of a Circle

Standard Form — Equation of a Circle

A circle with center $(h, k)$ and radius $r$ has the equation:

$$(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$$

This comes directly from the distance formula: every point $(x, y)$ on the circle is at distance $r$ from center $(h, k)$.

Example 1 — Writing the Equation

Write the equation of the circle with center $(3, -2)$ and radius $5$.

$(x - 3)^2 + (y - (-2))^2 = 5^2$

$$\boxed{(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 25}$$

Example 2 — Completing the Square

Find the center and radius of $x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 4y - 3 = 0$.

Group and complete the square:

$(x^2 - 6x \phantom{+9}) + (y^2 + 4y \phantom{+4}) = 3$

$(x^2 - 6x + 9) + (y^2 + 4y + 4) = 3 + 9 + 4$

$(x - 3)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 16$

Center: $(3, -2)$, Radius: $r = \sqrt{16} = 4$

Practice Problems

Unit 10 Practice — 12 Problems

  1. A tangent is drawn to a circle of radius 5 from an external point 13 units from the center. Find the length of the tangent segment.
  2. $PA$ and $PB$ are tangents from point $P$ to circle $O$. $PA = 5x - 3$ and $PB = 3x + 7$. Find $PB$.
  3. A central angle measures $110°$. Find the minor arc and major arc it intercepts.
  4. In a circle, $\overset{\frown}{AB} = 80°$ and $\overset{\frown}{BC} = 120°$. Find $\overset{\frown}{AC}$ (the minor arc not containing B).
  5. An inscribed angle intercepts an arc of $140°$. Find the inscribed angle.
  6. Two inscribed angles intercept the same arc of $96°$. What is each inscribed angle?
  7. A quadrilateral is inscribed in a circle. One angle is $82°$. Find the opposite angle.
  8. Two chords intersect inside a circle. The arcs they intercept measure $70°$ and $110°$. Find the angle formed.
  9. Two chords of lengths 6 and 8 intersect inside a circle. One chord is cut into segments of 2 and 6. Find the segments of the other chord.
  10. A secant from an external point passes through the circle with a whole length of 16 and an external segment of 4. A second secant has an external segment of 8. Find the whole length of the second secant.
  11. Write the equation of the circle with center $(-1, 3)$ passing through $(2, 3)$.
  12. Find the center and radius: $x^2 + y^2 + 8x - 10y + 16 = 0$.
