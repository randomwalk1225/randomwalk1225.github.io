Top 5 Detailed Profiles

1 University of Toronto

Canada's top-ranked university for mathematics and consistently ranked in the global top 20. UofT's Department of Mathematics is home to the Fields Institute for Research in Mathematical Sciences, one of the world's premier math research centres.

Key facts:
  • Admission: 90%+ average (top 6 courses), supplementary application for St. George campus
  • Programs: Mathematics Specialist, Major, Minor; Applied Math; Math & Physics; Math & Philosophy
  • Unique: Fields Institute partnership provides seminar access and research opportunities
  • Research: 60+ faculty across all major areas. Strong number theory, algebra, analysis
  • Co-op: Professional Experience Year (PEY) available

2 University of Waterloo

Waterloo's Faculty of Mathematics is the largest mathematics faculty in the world, with over 250 faculty members and 8,000+ students across its departments. It is uniquely known for its co-op programme, which alternates study and work terms.

Key facts:
  • Admission: 90%+ average + Euclid Math Contest score (strongly recommended)
  • Programs: Pure Math, Applied Math, Combinatorics & Optimization, Computational Math, Statistics, Actuarial Science, Math/Business, Math/CS
  • Unique: Co-op program — 6 paid work terms (Google, Amazon, banks, etc.)
  • Contests: CEMC (Centre for Education in Mathematics and Computing) — runs Euclid, CSMC, and more
  • Industry: Strongest industry connections of any Canadian math program

3 McGill University

McGill is consistently ranked as Canada's top university alongside UofT. Its Department of Mathematics and Statistics has a strong tradition in pure mathematics, with particular strengths in algebra, analysis, and mathematical physics.

Key facts:
  • Admission: 92%+ average for out-of-province; R-score based for Quebec CEGEP students
  • Programs: Honours Mathematics, Joint Honours Math-CS, Math-Physics, Statistics
  • Unique: Montreal's bilingual environment; CRM access; affordable Quebec tuition
  • Research: Strong algebra, analysis, and geometry groups
  • Culture: European-style academic rigour with North American flexibility

4 University of British Columbia (UBC)

UBC's mathematics department benefits from its partnership with the Pacific Institute for the Mathematical Sciences (PIMS), which connects researchers across Western Canada. The department is particularly strong in applied mathematics and interdisciplinary research.

Key facts:
  • Admission: 88%+ average for direct entry to Science
  • Programs: Mathematics Major/Honours, Combined Major (Math-CS, Math-Physics), Applied Math
  • Unique: PIMS centre for collaborative research; Co-op available; beautiful campus
  • Research: Applied math, probability, number theory, mathematical biology
  • Location: Vancouver — growing tech hub with industry opportunities

5 University of Alberta

The University of Alberta's Department of Mathematical and Statistical Sciences is one of the largest in Western Canada. It offers strong research programmes in mathematical physics, number theory, and applied mathematics.

Key facts:
  • Admission: 85%+ average for Science admission
  • Programs: Mathematics, Applied Math, Statistics, Honours programs available
  • Unique: PIMS member institution; strong funding support; affordable tuition
  • Research: Mathematical physics, number theory, differential equations
  • Value: Lower cost of living than Toronto, Vancouver, or Montreal

Waterloo Math Contests & Admissions

The University of Waterloo's CEMC (Centre for Education in Mathematics and Computing) runs Canada's most prestigious math competitions. Performance on these contests plays a significant role in Waterloo math admissions:

ContestGrade LevelImpact on Admissions
Euclid Mathematics ContestGrade 12High impact — top scores virtually guarantee admission
Canadian Senior Mathematics ContestGrade 11–12Moderate impact — demonstrates math ability
Canadian Open Mathematics ChallengeAll gradesLower impact but shows interest
Fermat/Cayley/PascalGrade 11/10/9Used for scholarship consideration
Euclid tip: A score of 80+ out of 100 on the Euclid contest (top ~5% nationally) significantly strengthens your Waterloo application. Many students scoring 85+ receive admission offers regardless of their school average. Study past Euclid contests — they are freely available on the CEMC website.

International Student Guide

Canada is one of the most popular destinations for international math students, offering high-quality education at lower cost than the US or UK:

Post-graduation work: Canada's Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows international students to work in Canada for up to 3 years after graduation — one of the most generous policies globally. This is a significant advantage over US and UK programmes.

Co-op & Industry Connections

Canadian universities, especially Waterloo, are known for strong co-op programmes that connect math students with industry:

  • Waterloo Co-op: 6 paid work terms alternating with study. Top employers: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Bloomberg, Jane Street, Citadel, major banks.
  • UofT PEY: 12–16 month Professional Experience Year between 3rd and 4th year.
  • UBC Co-op: Optional co-op with 4–5 work terms.
  • SFU Co-op: Strong co-op programme with Vancouver tech industry connections.
