Course Overview

BC Pre-Calculus 12 (formerly called Principles of Mathematics 12) is the highest-level Grade 12 mathematics course in the British Columbia curriculum. It is the standard prerequisite for first-year calculus at BC universities (UBC, SFU, UVIC, UVic, etc.) and is required for admission to engineering, mathematics, science, and economics programmes.

The course builds on Pre-Calculus 11 and covers topics that directly prepare students for university calculus. It is assessed through school-based exams and formerly through a BC provincial exam (the provincial exam requirement was modified in 2020 — check with your school for the current assessment structure).

University prerequisite: UBC Engineering and Science require Pre-Calculus 12 with a minimum 70% (or equivalent) for admission. Most BC university programmes require it as a prerequisite for MATH 100/110 (Calculus 1). UBC also offers a free Calculus Readiness Self-Test to help you gauge preparation.

Pre-Calculus 12 covers six major topic areas:

Highest-Priority Areas

Trigonometry and Exponentials/Logarithms together account for roughly 40% of assessments and are also the most heavily tested topics in university Calculus 1. Students who are weak in these areas tend to struggle significantly in first-year calculus.

Exam Tips

  • Trigonometry: know the unit circle cold. The exact values for sin and cos at 0, π/6, π/4, π/3, π/2, π and their extensions must be automatic. Do not rely on memorising tables — derive them from the 30-60-90 and 45-45-90 triangles.
  • Logarithm laws — write out each step. Many errors in log problems come from skipping steps. Always write log(ab) = log(a) + log(b) explicitly before simplifying.
  • Polynomial graphing — use sign charts. For multiplying factors like (x−2)²(x+1)(x−3), create a sign chart to determine the graph's behaviour near each root.
  • Transformations — identify order of operations. For y = af(b(x−h))+k, apply transformations in the correct order: horizontal compression/stretch → reflection → horizontal shift → vertical stretch → vertical shift.

Calculus 12 vs Pre-Calculus 12: BC also offers Calculus 12 as an elective at some schools. This is not a prerequisite for university calculus, but students who complete it typically perform better in MATH 100/110. Pre-Calculus 12 remains the standard university admission prerequisite.

Preparation Resources

  • BC Ministry curriculum documents: The official learning outcomes are publicly available — useful for checking your topic coverage before exams.
  • Past school final exams: Many BC schools post past final exam papers on their websites. These closely match what you will face.
  • Khan Academy: Free, well-structured video lessons for every Pre-Calculus 12 topic.
  • Paul's Online Math Notes (Lamar University): Excellent free resource for pre-calculus and calculus, with worked examples at exactly the right level.
  • MathHub Canada textbooks: Our Canada section includes worked examples and problem sets aligned with the BC curriculum.
Best preparation for university calculus: After completing Pre-Calculus 12, work through the first chapter of a university calculus textbook (Stewart Calculus or similar) over the summer before starting university. Students who preview limits and differentiation before September typically score one full letter grade higher in MATH 100.
