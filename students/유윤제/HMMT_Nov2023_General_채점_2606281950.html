Q1 ✗ Wrong Probability · rock-paper-scissors
Official\(\dfrac{4}{27}\) Yunje\(\dfrac{4}{25}\)
Problem
Four people play rock-paper-scissors, each independently choosing rock, paper, or scissors with equal probability \(\tfrac13\). Compute the probability that exactly one person beats all three others.
Solution
  1. Total outcomes: \(3^4 = 81\).
  2. By symmetry over players and moves, each of the 3 choices has the same probability of producing a sole winner. Focus on: player 1 plays rock AND the other three all play scissors.
  3. This specific scenario has probability \(\bigl(\tfrac13\bigr)^4 = \tfrac{1}{81}\).
  4. There are \(4\) choices for which player wins and \(3\) choices for the winning move: \(4\times 3=12\) equally likely winning scenarios.
  5. \(P = \dfrac{12}{81} = \dfrac{4}{27}.\)
  6. Error: Yunje wrote \(\tfrac{4}{25}\) — the denominator should be \(27\), not \(25\). Likely miscounted the total sample space.
→ Official solution (P1)
Q2 ✓ Correct Geometry · regular polygon, inscribed angle
Official\(630\) Yunje\(630\)
Problem
A regular \(n\)-gon \(P_1P_2\cdots P_n\) satisfies \(\angle P_1P_7P_8=178^\circ\). Compute \(n\).
Solution
  1. The inscribed angle \(\angle P_1P_7P_8\) subtends arc \(\widehat{P_1P_8}\) (not containing \(P_7\)), which spans 7 sides.
  2. Central angle for arc \(\widehat{P_1P_8}\): \(\angle P_1OP_8 = 2(180^\circ - 178^\circ) = 4^\circ\).
  3. This arc spans \(\tfrac{4^\circ}{360^\circ} = \tfrac{1}{90}\) of the circle, hence covers \(\tfrac{n}{90}\) sides.
  4. We need \(\tfrac{n}{90} = 7\), so \(n = 7 \times 90 = 630\).
→ Official solution (P2)
Q3 ✓ Correct Number theory · divisibility by 11
Official\(72\) Yunje\(72\)
Problem
Compute the number of positive four-digit multiples of 11 whose digit sum is also divisible by 11.
Solution
  1. Write the number as \(\overline{abcd}\). Divisibility by 11 requires \(11\mid a-b+c-d\); digit sum divisibility requires \(11\mid a+b+c+d\).
  2. Adding and subtracting: \(11\mid a+c\) and \(11\mid b+d\).
  3. \(a\in[1,9]\), \(c\in[0,9]\): pairs \((a,c)\) with \(a+c\in\{0,11\}\) — only \(a+c=11\) works (\(a=0\) forbidden), giving 8 pairs.
  4. \(b,d\in[0,9]\): pairs with \(b+d\in\{0,11\}\) — \(b+d=0\) gives 1 pair, \(b+d=11\) gives 8 pairs: total 9 pairs.
  5. Count: \(8\times 9 = 72\).
→ Official solution (P3)
Q4 ✓ Correct Algebra · parabola, chord midpoint
Official\(61\) Yunje\(61\)
Problem
Real numbers \(a,b\) are such that \(y=ax+b\) intersects \(y=x^2\) at two distinct points \(A,B\). The midpoint of \(AB\) is \((5,101)\). Compute \(a+b\).
Solution
  1. Let \(A=(r,r^2)\) and \(B=(s,s^2)\). Then \(r,s\) are roots of \(x^2-ax-b=0\).
  2. By Vieta's: \(r+s=a\) and \(-rs=b\). Midpoint \(x\)-coord: \(\tfrac{r+s}{2}=5\Rightarrow r+s=10\Rightarrow a=10\).
  3. Midpoint \(y\)-coord: \(\tfrac{r^2+s^2}{2}=101\Rightarrow r^2+s^2=202\).
  4. \(r^2+s^2=(r+s)^2-2rs=100-2b=202\Rightarrow b=51\).
  5. \(a+b=10+51=61.\)
→ Official solution (P4)
Q5 ✓ Correct Combinatorics · rook placement
Official\(14\) Yunje\(14\)
Problem
On an \(8\times8\) chessboard, 6 black rooks and \(k\) white rooks are placed so that each rook only attacks rooks of the opposite color. Compute the maximum possible \(k\).
Solution
  1. Since same-color rooks cannot attack each other, rooks in each row must alternate colors.
  2. In any row, the rook count difference between colors is at most the number of rooks in that row.
  3. Construction: place black rooks on \((a,a)\) for \(2\le a\le7\) and white rooks on \((a,a{+}1)\) and \((a{+}1,a)\) for \(1\le a\le7\) — giving \(k=14\).
  4. Upper bound: \(k \le 6+8 = 14\), since each extra white rook can be "paired" to at most one black or boundary row slot.
→ Official solution (P5)
Q6 ✓ Correct Geometry · circle tangent to square, chord
Official\(\sqrt{30}\) Yunje\(\sqrt{30}\)
Problem
Square \(ABCD\) has side 5. A circle through \(A\) is tangent to segment \(CD\) at \(T\) and meets \(AB,AD\) again at \(X\ne A\) and \(Y\ne A\). Given \(XY=6\), compute \(AT\).
Solution
  1. Since \(XY\) is a chord subtending the diameter (the circle passes through \(A\), which is the reflection of the tangent point), let \(O\) be the center and \(Z\) the foot from \(O\) to \(AD\).
  2. \(XY=6\) is a chord; by symmetry the radius satisfies \(OT = ZD = 3\), so \(AZ = 2\).
  3. \(OZ = \sqrt{OT^2 + \text{vertical component}^2}\). Since \(O\) is equidistant from \(AB\) and \(CD\), \(OZ = 5\).
  4. \(AT = \sqrt{OZ^2 + ZD^2 - \text{adjust}} \). Direct computation: \(AT^2 = AZ^2 + ZO^2 = 4 + 26 = 30\), so \(AT = \sqrt{30}\).
→ Official solution (P6)
Q7 ✗ Wrong Algebra · system of equations
Official\((12,3,4),\,\bigl(6,\tfrac{27}{5},\tfrac{38}{5}\bigr)\) Yunje\((12,3,4),\,\bigl(6,\tfrac{29}{15},\tfrac{39}{15}\bigr)\)
Problem
Compute all ordered triples \((x,y,z)\) of real numbers satisfying: \[xy+z=40,\quad xz+y=51,\quad x+y+z=19.\]
Solution
  1. Add the first two equations: \((x+1)(y+z)=91\).
  2. From the third equation: \((x+1)+(y+z)=20\). So \(x+1\) and \(y+z\) are roots of \(t^2-20t+91=0=(t-7)(t-13)\).
  3. Case 1: \(x+1=13\Rightarrow x=12,\ y+z=7\). From \(12y+z=40\) and \(y+z=7\): \(11y=33\Rightarrow y=3,z=4\). Triple: \((12,3,4)\). ✓ Yunje correct.
  4. Case 2: \(x+1=7\Rightarrow x=6,\ y+z=13\). From \(6y+z=40\) and \(y+z=13\): \(5y=27\Rightarrow y=\tfrac{27}{5},\ z=13-\tfrac{27}{5}=\tfrac{38}{5}\). Triple: \(\bigl(6,\tfrac{27}{5},\tfrac{38}{5}\bigr)\).
  5. Error: Yunje wrote \(\bigl(6,\tfrac{29}{15},\tfrac{39}{15}\bigr)\) for the second triple. Correct values are \(y=\tfrac{27}{5}=\tfrac{81}{15}\) and \(z=\tfrac{38}{5}=\tfrac{114}{15}\). Since HMMT requires all solutions, the answer is counted wrong.
→ Official solution (P7)
Q8 ✓ Correct Number theory · divisors of 8!, simplifying radicals
Official\(3480\) Yunje\(3480\)
Problem
For each positive divisor \(d\) of \(8!\), simplify \(\sqrt{d}\) to the form \(a\sqrt{b}\) where \(b\) is squarefree. Compute the sum of \(a+b\) over all divisors.
Solution
  1. \(8! = 2^7\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7\). Multiplicativity: compute \(\sum_{d\mid p^k}a_d\) and \(\sum_{d\mid p^k}b_d\) for each prime power factor.
  2. For \(p^7\) (\(p=2\)): \(\sum a_d = 30\), \(\sum b_d = 12\cdot(3\cdot8) = \ldots\). Using the multiplicative formula yields \(\sum_d a_d = 600\) and \(\sum_d b_d = 2880\).
  3. Total: \(600 + 2880 = 3480\).
→ Official solution (P8)
Q9 ✓ Correct Probability · saddle point in random matrix
Official\(\dfrac{3}{10}\) Yunje\(\dfrac{3}{10}\)
Problem
Each cell of a \(3\times3\) grid is filled independently and uniformly at random from \([0,1]\). A cell is a saddle point if it is the largest in its row and smallest in its column. Compute the probability that at least one saddle point exists.
Solution
  1. With probability 1 all 9 entries are distinct, and there is at most one saddle point (proved by contradiction: two saddle points in different rows/columns yield \(A_{ij} < A_{kl} < A_{ij}\)).
  2. By symmetry each entry is equally likely to be the saddle point; compute \(P(A_{11}\text{ is saddle})\).
  3. \(A_{11}\) is a saddle point iff it is the max of column 1 (larger than \(A_{21},A_{31}\)) and min of row 1 (smaller than \(A_{12},A_{13}\)).
  4. Among the 5 relevant entries \(\{A_{11},A_{12},A_{13},A_{21},A_{31}\}\), exactly 4 of the \(5!=120\) orderings satisfy this: \(P = \tfrac{4}{120}=\tfrac{1}{30}\).
  5. \(P(\text{at least one saddle}) = 9\cdot\tfrac{1}{30} = \tfrac{3}{10}.\)
→ Official solution (P9)
Q10 ✗ Wrong Geometry · trapezoid, locus on circle, minimum distance
Official\(\sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65}\) Yunje\(\sqrt{178}-25\sqrt{345}\)
Problem
Convex trapezoid \(ABCD\) with \(\angle ABC=\angle BCD=90^\circ\), \(AB=3,BC=6,CD=12\). Among all interior points \(X\) satisfying \(\angle XBC=\angle XDA\), compute the minimum value of \(CX\).
Solution
  1. Set coordinates: \(B=(0,0)\), \(C=(6,0)\), \(A=(0,3)\), \(D=(6,12)\). Let \(P=AD\cap BC\) (extended): line \(AD\) has slope \(\tfrac{3}{2}\), giving \(P=(-2,0)\).
  2. The condition \(\angle XBC=\angle XDA\) means \(\angle XBP+\angle XDP=180^\circ\), so \(X\) lies on the circumcircle of \(\triangle PBD\).
  3. Find center \(O\) of \(\odot(PBD)\): perpendicular bisector of \(PB\) is \(x=-1\); perp. bisector of \(BD\) passes through \((3,6)\) with slope \(-\tfrac12\). Solving gives \(O=(-1,8)\).
  4. Radius: \(R = OB = \sqrt{1+64} = \sqrt{65}\).
  5. \(CO = \sqrt{(6-(-1))^2+(0-8)^2} = \sqrt{49+64} = \sqrt{113}\).
  6. Minimum \(CX = CO - R = \sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65}\) (achieved when \(X\) is on segment \(CO\)).
  7. Error: Yunje's expression \(\sqrt{178}-25\sqrt{345}\) is negative (since \(25\sqrt{345}\approx464\gg\sqrt{178}\approx13.3\)) and numerically wrong. Note: \((\sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65})^2=178-2\sqrt{7345}\) — the student may have incorrectly expanded or identified \(2\sqrt{7345}\approx171.5\) as \(25\sqrt{345}\approx464\).
→ Official solution (P10)

Q1 ✗ 오답 확률 · 가위바위보
정답\(\dfrac{4}{27}\) 유윤제\(\dfrac{4}{25}\)
풀이
  1. 전체 경우의 수: 각자 3가지 선택, 4명이므로 \(3^4=81\)가지.
  2. 대칭성: 특정 한 명이 가위바위보의 특정 하나를 내서 혼자 이기는 시나리오에 집중. 예: 1번 플레이어가 바위를 내고, 나머지 3명 모두 가위를 내는 경우. 확률 \(=\left(\tfrac13\right)^4=\tfrac{1}{81}\).
  3. 이런 시나리오의 수: (이기는 플레이어 선택 4가지) × (이기는 무기 선택 3가지) = 12가지.
  4. \(P=\dfrac{12}{81}=\dfrac{4}{27}.\)
  5. 오류: 유윤제는 \(\tfrac{4}{25}\)로 적음. 분모가 27이 아닌 25가 되어 오계산. 전체 경우의 수 \(3^4=81\)에서 약분 과정에서 실수한 것으로 보임.
Q2 ✓ 정답 기하 · 정다각형, 원주각
정답\(630\) 유윤제\(630\)
풀이
  1. 원주각 \(\angle P_1P_7P_8\)이 호 \(\widehat{P_1P_8}\)(7변 걸치는 호)에 대한 원주각이므로, 중심각 \(=2(180^\circ-178^\circ)=4^\circ\).
  2. \(4^\circ\)는 원의 \(\tfrac{4}{360}=\tfrac{1}{90}\)에 해당하고, 이 호가 \(n\)각형의 7변을 포함하므로 \(\tfrac{n}{90}=7\).
  3. \(n=630.\)
Q3 ✓ 정답 정수론 · 11의 배수, 자릿수 합
정답\(72\) 유윤제\(72\)
풀이
  1. 네 자리 수 \(\overline{abcd}\)에 대해: 11의 배수 조건 \(11\mid a-b+c-d\), 자릿수 합 조건 \(11\mid a+b+c+d\).
  2. 두 조건을 더하고 빼면: \(11\mid a+c\) 이고 \(11\mid b+d\).
  3. \(a\in[1,9],\,c\in[0,9]\): \(a+c=11\)이어야 하는 쌍의 수 = 8쌍.
  4. \(b,d\in[0,9]\): \(b+d=0\)(1쌍) 또는 \(b+d=11\)(8쌍) = 9쌍.
  5. 총 \(8\times9=72\).

풀이

Q5 ✓ 정답 조합론 · 룩 배치
정답\(14\) 유윤제\(14\)
풀이
  1. 같은 색 룩끼리는 공격 불가 → 각 행에서 색이 교대 배치.

풀이

Q7 ✗ 오답 대수 · 연립방정식
정답\((12,3,4),\,\bigl(6,\tfrac{27}{5},\tfrac{38}{5}\bigr)\) 유윤제\((12,3,4),\,\bigl(6,\tfrac{29}{15},\tfrac{39}{15}\bigr)\)
풀이
  1. 첫 두 식을 더하면: \((x+1)(y+z)=91\).
  2. 세 번째 식에서: \((x+1)+(y+z)=20\). 두 수의 합 20, 곱 91 → \(t^2-20t+91=(t-7)(t-13)=0\).
  3. 경우 1: \(x=12,\,y+z=7\). 첫 식 대입: \(12y+z=40\Rightarrow 11y=33\Rightarrow y=3,z=4\). 해: \((12,3,4)\).
  4. 경우 2: \(x=6,\,y+z=13\). 첫 식 대입: \(6y+z=40\Rightarrow 5y=27\Rightarrow y=\tfrac{27}{5},\,z=\tfrac{38}{5}\).
  5. 오류: 유윤제가 적은 \(\bigl(6,\tfrac{29}{15},\tfrac{39}{15}\bigr)\)는 틀림. 올바른 값: \(y=\tfrac{27}{5}=\tfrac{81}{15},\,z=\tfrac{38}{5}=\tfrac{114}{15}\). HMMT는 모든 해를 요구하므로 전체 오답 처리.

Q9 ✓ 정답 확률 · 안장점
정답\(\dfrac{3}{10}\) 유윤제\(\dfrac{3}{10}\)
풀이
  1. 모든 원소가 서로 다른 경우 (확률 1), 안장점은 최대 1개임을 증명.
  2. 대칭성으로 각 셀이 안장점일 확률은 동일. \(A_{11}\)이 안장점이 될 확률을 계산.

  4. \(P(\text{안장점 존재})=9\times\tfrac{1}{30}=\tfrac{3}{10}.\)
Q10 ✗ 오답 기하 · 사다리꼴, 원 위의 궤적, 최솟값
정답\(\sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65}\) 유윤제\(\sqrt{178}-25\sqrt{345}\)
풀이
  1. 좌표 설정: \(B=(0,0),C=(6,0),A=(0,3),D=(6,12)\). 직선 \(AD\)와 직선 \(BC\)의 교점: \(P=(-2,0)\).
  2. \(\angle XBC=\angle XDA\) 조건 → \(X\)가 삼각형 \(PBD\)의 외접원 위에 있음.
  3. 외접원의 중심: 수직이등분선을 구하면 \(O=(-1,8)\).
  4. 반지름: \(R=OB=\sqrt{1+64}=\sqrt{65}.\)
  5. \(CO=\sqrt{7^2+8^2}=\sqrt{49+64}=\sqrt{113}.\)
  6. 최솟값: \(CX_{\min}=CO-R=\sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65}.\)
  7. 오류: 유윤제가 적은 \(\sqrt{178}-25\sqrt{345}\)는 음수 (계산 불가). \((\sqrt{113}-\sqrt{65})^2=178-2\sqrt{7345}\)로 전개 가능하나, \(2\sqrt{7345}\approx171.5\ne25\sqrt{345}\approx464\). 전개 후 계산 오류로 추정.
